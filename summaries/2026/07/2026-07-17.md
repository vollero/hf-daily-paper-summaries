# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-17

## Executive summary
- The day splits between agent/robot learning, multimodal generation/evaluation, video understanding, and inference/training efficiency.
- Two papers focus on on-policy distillation: `2607.14777` applies OPD-style hindsight skills to long-horizon agents, while `2607.13399` studies OPD pathologies and regulation in math reasoning.
- Reference-conditioned generation evaluation is a clear cluster: `2607.14202` tests keyframe-conditioned video, and `2607.14189` tests multi-reference audio-video generation.
- Robotics papers emphasize robustness and context: `2607.15207` attacks world-action models, while `2607.15275` scales robot policy context to thousands of timesteps.
- Video/visual reasoning papers move in different directions: `2607.14935` targets efficient open video understanding, `2607.12800` trains visual reasoning trajectories, and `2607.13188` couples text-image masked diffusion sampling.
- Several systems externalize reusable state: `2607.15257` for search-agent memory, `2607.14431` for verified KV-cache reuse, and `2607.15275` for robot fast-weight history.
- Generative-model alignment appears in audio, image, and video: `2607.14749` reports long-form song generation, `2607.15273` adapts forward-process RL to MeanFlow, and `2607.15038` frames streaming video as world plus event stream.
- `2607.13491` is the day’s singleton: a theory-and-systems paper on residual scaling for looped Transformers.

## Main results worth noting
- `2607.14777`: SEED reports gains over GRPO across ALFWorld, Search QA, and WebShop; with 60% ALFWorld data, SEED scores `80.7`, above full-data GRPO at `75.0`.
- `2607.15257`: SearchOS reports WideSearch Item F1 `80.3` versus A-MapReduce `76.0`, and GISA set F1 `76.5` versus best baseline `63.1`, under the paper’s Max@3 setting.
- `2607.15207`: BadWAM action-only attack reduces action-only WAM LIBERO success from `96.5%` to `43.1%`; simple augmentation-consistency detectors catch only `13.4%` and `21.4%` of attacked replans at `5%` FPR for two WAMs.
- `2607.14202`: Keyframe fidelity and video quality diverge: LTX-2.3 has Keyframe Fidelity `0.855`, while Gemini-Omni-Flash has Video Quality `0.861` but lower Keyframe Fidelity `0.483`.
- `2607.14935`: VideoChat3 at `2048` frames uses `100,352` visual tokens vs Qwen3-VL `200,704`, with latency `20.412s` vs `44.449s` on the reported H200 setup.
- `2607.12800`: UniVR improves VR-X overall from `39.8` to `58.2` over Emu3.5; Gemini-3-pro remains higher overall at `66.1`, while UniVR slightly leads on the robot task `68.0` vs `67.1`.
- `2607.14749`: WanSong reports PER `7.43%` on WanSong Bench, but the benchmark and musicality evaluator are internal.
- `2607.15275`: RoboTTT reports `79%` average task completion versus `42%` for GR00T N1.7 and `56%` for GDN across three assembly tasks.
- `2607.15273`: MeanFlowNFT’s 4-step SD3.5-M results exceed AnyFlow on most listed metrics, including ImageReward `1.4504` vs `1.1125` and OCR `0.6534` vs `0.4520`.
- `2607.14431`: KV grafting reports byte-exact own-position reuse with median/p99 KL `0.0`, plus cold prefill `1,547.3 ms` versus warm graft `18.1 ms`.

## Open research directions
1. Stress-test self-generated supervision under controlled noise: `2607.14777`, `2607.13399`. Inject incorrect hindsight skills or distorted teacher token advantages and measure downstream agent/math performance.
2. Build stronger evidence for automated generation judges: `2607.14202`, `2607.14189`, `2607.13188`, `2607.12800`. Run blinded human studies across all benchmark strata and compare rank stability against multiple MLLM judges.
3. Connect robot robustness and recovery: `2607.15207`, `2607.15275`. Evaluate whether long-context fast-weight policies recover from BadWAM-style perturbations better than short-context policies under matched closed-loop tasks.
4. Isolate context-memory components: `2607.15257`, `2607.14431`, `2607.15275`. Ablate frontier/evidence/failure memory, KV block routing, and fast-weight history separately under matched long-horizon workloads.
5. Test visual trajectory reasoning in real control loops: `2607.12800`, `2607.15275`. Feed UniVR-style visual plans into robot policies and measure physical task success, recovery, and plan-policy mismatch.
6. Validate reward alignment across modalities: `2607.15273`, `2607.14749`, `2607.13188`. Compare reward models and human ratings for image, video, and song generation under matched RL budgets.
7. Measure generality of efficient video/context systems: `2607.14935`, `2607.15038`, `2607.14431`. Sweep latency, frame count, concurrency, and streaming domains while preserving quality metrics.
8. Empirically test DeepLoop’s mechanism: `2607.13491`. Log visit-wise gradient and sensitivity alignment to estimate `kappa_R`, then sweep residual exponents at larger model scales and loop counts.
