# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-24

## Executive summary

- Agent evaluation is a major theme. WorkBuddy Bench (`2607.20911`) shows that coding-agent scores and rankings shift with the harness, while the evolving-intent framework (`2607.20734`) finds that progressively revised requirements can sharply degrade otherwise strong single-turn performance.
- Two papers consolidate accumulated experience rather than repeatedly replaying raw histories: AREX (`2607.21461`) compresses research progress into an inference-time improvement state, while Experience Distillation (`2607.21051`) transfers experience-conditioned decisions into model weights.
- Embodied systems emphasize language-conditioned monocular control. ReferTrack (`2607.20061`) grounds a described pedestrian before planning tracking waypoints; Robostral Navigate (`2607.20785`) predicts navigation waypoints from instructions and RGB history.
- Structured conditioning also appears in video generation. WorldWeaver (`2607.21594`) maintains persistent cross-agent state registers, whereas GraphVid (`2607.21580`) uses editable directed interaction graphs.
- Visual Contrastive Self-Distillation (`2607.21556`) creates self-teaching targets by comparing an EMA model under original and content-erased images, reporting consistent aggregate gains in the tested Qwen/ViRL39K setting.
- The collection includes several specialized representation and sensing contributions: camera–display color coupling (`2607.12746`), recurrent sinusoidal implicit representations (`2607.21485`), and predictive divergence masks for off-policy LLM RL (`2607.10848`).
- Dataset and evaluation work extends beyond agents: ProVisE (`2607.21072`) parses generated pixels into native spatial metrics, while TableVerse (`2607.21017`) converts single images into physically rectified tabletop simulations.
- Most headline evidence comes from benchmarks or simulation. Real-robot demonstrations, cross-domain transfer, statistical uncertainty, compute accounting, and public-artifact availability are frequently incomplete.

## Main results worth noting

- **Recursive research:** AREX (`2607.21461`) scored 82.5 on BrowseComp under up to 300 inner turns and five outer operations. In matched BrowseComp ablations, autonomous context updating changed 59.6 to 71.4, while the outer loop changed 71.4 to 82.5 when context updating was enabled.

- **Visual self-distillation:** VCSD (`2607.21556`) improved the reported seven-benchmark aggregate over matched OPSD across all six tested Qwen configurations trained on ViRL39K. For Qwen3-VL-2B, the reported comparison was 62.27% versus 67.04%; forward KL scored 67.04%, 0.79 points above JSD and 2.27 above reverse KL in that setting.

- **Embodied tracking:** ReferTrack (`2607.20061`) reported single-forward-camera EVT-Bench SR/TR/CR of 89.4/92.5/1.6 on STT, 73.3/81.8/7.6 on DT, and 74.1/85.7/7.7 on AT. On DT, oracle target boxes raised SR from 73.3% to 81.5%, indicating remaining identification headroom.

- **Harness sensitivity:** WorkBuddy (`2607.20911`) found an 8.6-point mean absolute Security-score shift across seven models between CodeBuddy Code and Claude Code. On Code, GPT-5.5 versus GLM-5.2 changed from 72.90 versus 71.54 under one harness to 76.63 versus 77.06 under the other.

- **Pixel-output evaluation:** On ProVisE’s (`2607.21072`) 470-sample, 14-task macro-average, GPT Image 2 scored 54.49, GPT-5.4 scored 61.04, and humans scored 87.79. Parser choice itself shifted mean scores by 5.26–5.87 points for the three tested universal VLM parsers.

- **Color reproduction:** Color Pass-Through (`2607.12746`) reports a +2.0-point mean advantage on a five-point perceptual scale in a ten-person study covering ten unseen scenes. The paper also reports more than twofold improvement on quantitative metrics, but exact values and the defining baseline are unavailable.

- **Implicit representations:** Recurrent Sinusoidal INRs (`2607.21485`) raised step-500 image-fitting PSNR from 39.724 dB with one pass to 63.378 dB with five recurrent steps at fixed parameter count. In pretrained SR with roughly 50K decoder parameters, Set5 ×2 improved from 37.673/0.9485 to 37.721/0.9489 PSNR/SSIM, but Set5 ×4 LPIPS worsened from 0.1797 to 0.1867.

- **Evolving intent:** Under six controlled transitions, `2607.20734` reports GPT-5.5 GSM8K accuracy falling from 99.0% to 80.5%, and DeepSeek V3.2 BrowseComp+ accuracy from 36.0% to 15.0%. An oracle recap raised GPT-5.5 BIRD-SQL function-switch accuracy from 65% to 75%, still below its 80% single-turn result.

- **Tabletop reconstruction:** TableVerse (`2607.21017`) reduced pre-relaxation scene collision rate from 79.0% with direct alignment to 0.0% with LCCR over 100 images. Its 7.14/7.08/7.03 layout, visual, and geometry scores were assigned by an MLLM, not human raters.

- **Multi-agent world modeling:** WorldWeaver (`2607.21594`) raised Solaris category-level VLM accuracy from 81.3 to 93.8 for Grounding, 9.4 to 28.1 for Building, and 57.8 to 76.6 for Consistency. With 1K labeled clips, adding 10K unlabeled clips increased the reported WorldScore from 63.2 to 90.3.

- **Navigation:** Robostral Navigate (`2607.20785`) reported R2R-CE validation-unseen NE 3.20 m, OS 81.3%, SR 77.4%, and SPL 74.2. Its SFT-to-RL comparison increased validation-unseen SR from 73.40% to 77.43%; the joint contribution of CISPO and associated data-selection choices is not isolated.

- **Object-oriented agents:** NOOA (`2607.20709`) reached 82.2% on SWE-bench Verified with GPT-5.5/xhigh, compared with 78.6% for OpenCode and 78.2% for PI under the reported configurations. Separately, structured memory improved GPT-5.5 ARC-AGI-3 RHAE from 38.4% to 50.2% in a 25-game fleet.

- **Off-policy RL direction tests:** Predictive Divergence Masks (`2607.10848`) lowered unsafe keeps from 36.9% to 34.2% on disagreement tokens outside the trust region across 61 seeds. These disagreements averaged only about 82 tokens per seed out of 1,435 out-of-region tokens, and exact end-to-end AIME deltas were not supplied.

- **Experience consolidation:** Experience Distillation (`2607.21051`) reached 51.4% pass@1 on 749 SWE tasks, versus 8.0% for direct SFT and 17.7% for the evaluated PPO configuration; the PPO run used 9.6× more complete rollouts. On 494 held-out SWE tasks, pass@1 rose from 4.62% to 8.84%.

- **Graph-controlled video:** GraphVid (`2607.21580`) achieved FID/FVD of 17.02/99.42 on the interaction-focused MoveBench subset, compared with 28.32/159.32 for Motion-I2V and 15.56/82.17 for the substantially larger Wan-Move. In a 1,200-judgment study, GraphVid received 39.5% of semantic-intent votes.

## Open research directions

1. **Factor harness effects into causal components** (`2607.20911`, `2607.20709`). Run the same models and tasks while independently varying typed termination, context rendering, live references, tool schemas, memory, and prompt wrappers; measure score and ranking stability.

2. **Test evolving intent on realistic workspace tasks and human conversations** (`2607.20911`, `2607.20734`). Transform verifier-backed WorkBuddy tasks, then collect matched synthetic and human-authored conversations with the same final verifiers and compare transition-specific degradation.

3. **Separate grounding from control in monocular robotics** (`2607.20061`, `2607.20785`). Under matched backbones and scenes, compare indexed-box grounding, pixel waypoints, metric waypoints, and their combinations while injecting detector errors and camera-calibration shifts.

4. **Measure structured video control directly** (`2607.21594`, `2607.21580`, `2607.21072`). Build frame-linked parsers for requested relations, contacts, and trajectories; report exact compliance, physical violations, identity consistency, and drift by rollout horizon.

5. **Test endogenous distillation across architectures and modalities** (`2607.21556`, `2607.21051`). Compare extra visual context, interaction history, and other conditioning signals across unrelated model families under matched teacher-generation FLOPs and environment budgets.

6. **Determine what history must be preserved** (`2607.21461`, `2607.21051`). Remove or perturb verified findings, rejected attempts, unresolved constraints, provenance, and feedback events separately; measure later decisions, transfer, and failure recurrence.

7. **Establish real-world embodied validity** (`2607.20061`, `2607.21017`, `2607.20785`). Report repeated physical trials, safety events, trajectory execution success, target-loss recovery, and sim-to-real gaps rather than qualitative demonstrations alone.

8. **Evaluate representation quality under compute parity** (`2607.12746`, `2607.21485`). For camera–display correction, test held-out devices and difficult spectra; for recurrent INRs, match FLOPs and wall time against independently parameterized depth across 2D and 3D tasks.

9. **Validate local RL predictions against exact updates** (`2607.10848`). Store full-vocabulary distributions in controlled runs, compare exact and top-K derivative signs, and repeat end-to-end training across seeds, learning rates, batch sizes, domains, and divergence thresholds.

10. **Close evaluation loops with human and deterministic checks** (`2607.21072`, `2607.21017`, `2607.21580`). Compare parser- and MLLM-based scores with blinded human judgments and task-native physical or geometric verification, including ranking stability across evaluators.

## Coverage and uncertainty

All 15 supplied current papers were analyzed and appear in the taxonomy or singleton notes, the at-a-glance table, and the Sources section.

The taxonomy and same-day connection stages were available and were checked against the supplied paper analyses. Some connections are explicitly framed as compatible experiments or follow-up opportunities rather than author-reported demonstrations.

The historical-connections stage was unavailable. A collection failure also reports that an attempted historical connection referenced current paper `2607.20709` as though it were a previous paper. Consequently, no historical edge or historical source was retained.

Full Hugging Face Markdown collection failed for `2607.20734`, `2607.10848`, `2607.21051`, and `2607.21580`. Their supplied analyses nevertheless contained substantial paper evidence, but the collection failures reduce confidence in completeness. Other analyses frequently report missing rendered tables, including `2607.12746`, `2607.21485`, `2607.21594`, and parts of `2607.10848`.

Low-confidence or qualified conclusions include Color Pass-Through’s unspecified “more than 2×” quantitative improvement; end-to-end gains for Predictive Divergence Masks without exact endpoint scores; several efficiency claims without matched hardware or compute accounting; and physical-deployment claims supported only by qualitative demonstrations. Across the collection, statistical uncertainty, independent replication, and artifact availability are often absent.
