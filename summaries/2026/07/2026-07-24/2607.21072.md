# Show, Don&#x27;t Tell: Evaluating Spatial Cognition in Generative Pixels Rather Than LLM Text

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-24.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.21072) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.21072)


## One-sentence takeaway

ProVisE converts protocol-constrained visual answers into metric-compatible predictions, revealing that current image generators compete on directly externalizable spatial tasks but trail text-output VLMs on compositional reasoning.

## Problem and motivation

Spatial benchmarks usually demand coordinates, labels, or prose even when answers are naturally expressed by pointing, masking, or drawing. The authors argue that this interface mismatch prevents image-generation models from being evaluated alongside text-output VLMs under identical task semantics and metrics; post-hoc VLM judges are an inadequate substitute because their interpretation can alter scores and rankings (Introduction; Figure 1; Section 4.5).

## Approach and contributions

- **ProVisE:** A benchmark-agnostic pipeline pairing constrained visual-answer prompts with parsers that recover labels, counts, masks, points, states, or trajectories for scoring with the source metric (Sections 2.1–2.4; Figure 2).
- **Agentic builder:** Normalizes unfamiliar benchmarks into task contracts, then reuses, builds, or falls back to task-specific generation–parser protocols and smoke-validates them before freezing them across models (Section 2.2; Figure 3).
- **SpatialGen-Bench:** A curated 470-sample diagnostic suite with 14 subtasks organized into perception, understanding, reasoning, and interaction (Section 3; Figure 5).
- **Unified empirical comparison:** Evaluates 20 text-answering and 11 visual-answering systems, studies paired complementarity, parser sensitivity, failure attribution, and protocol transfer to six external benchmarks (Sections 4.1–4.6).

The evidence supports the authors’ claim that protocolized pixels can be evaluated with existing task metrics and can expose complementary successes. It does **not** establish a causal advantage of visual versus textual output: architectures, scales, training data, and sometimes decoding paths differ. The six external evaluations are transfer pilots, not full-benchmark rankings, and paired rescue rates are diagnostic rather than native leaderboard metrics.

## Limitations and validity risks

**Author-stated limitations:**

- Protocol construction uses GPT-5.4 and GPT Image 2, potentially favoring representations executable by GPT Image 2 (Section 4.7).
- Fallback parsing introduces provider-dependent variability and exposes incomplete deterministic-parser coverage (Section 4.7; Table 9 reports 8 of 23 external task units using Fallback).
- Text and visual model pools differ in architecture, scale, and training data, preventing causal attribution to output modality (Section 4.7).
- ProVisE currently covers static-image benchmarks with recoverable annotations and metrics, not video, simulation, or closed-loop embodied tasks (Section 4.7).
- Bootstrap intervals capture sample-selection uncertainty for cached outputs but not generation stochasticity or provider updates (Appendix E.5).

**Analyst inferences:**

- Protocol validation emphasizes executability rather than correctness, so passing smoke tests does not prove an unbiased or semantically optimal protocol (Appendix C.3).
- Some results depend on fixed auxiliary models, CLIP, OCR, or generated-image-only VLM parsers; parser sensitivity shows that these choices can affect comparisons (Table 8; Section 4.5).
- SpatialGen-Bench contains only 25–40 examples per task, yielding relatively broad uncertainty for several capability scores (Table 6; Table 18).
- The supplied text does not report a controlled same-model, same-training causal ablation beyond the partial SenseNova comparison, which the authors explicitly describe as descriptive (Figure 6).

## Open research questions

1. **Can modality effects be isolated from model-family differences?** The current pools are heterogeneous. A next experiment could evaluate a unified model with matched weights, inputs, decoding budget, and both answer interfaces across every task.

2. **Can deterministic protocol coverage replace fallback VLM parsing?** Eight of 23 external task units use Fallback. Expanding parser operators and comparing deterministic and fallback routes on identical saved outputs would measure coverage–reliability trade-offs.

3. **Does protocolized evaluation extend to temporal and closed-loop behavior?** The present scope is static images. A next experiment could define frame-linked protocols for video localization or action trajectories and evaluate them in a simulator with task-success metrics.

4. **Can hybrid systems exploit the observed complementarity?** Rescue analysis shows non-overlapping successes but does not test combination. A routing or cross-checking system could select text, pixels, or both per task and compare against each interface alone on frozen examples.

## Confidence and missing information

**Confidence: high.** The supplied text includes methods, complete benchmark composition, principal results, task-level scores, uncertainty estimates, parser rules, and stated limitations.

Material missing information includes the exact executable prompts and repository artifacts, full details of human-participant recruitment and annotation procedure, generation costs and latency, provider-side reproducibility guarantees, and experimental evidence for video or closed-loop embodied settings.
