# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-14

## Executive summary

- Two papers transfer reward-induced relative policy changes from smaller models to stronger ones rather than imitating the smaller model’s absolute policy: Direct-OPD (2607.05394) reports consistent AIME gains, while PUST (2607.11505) extends the pattern to math and code within Qwen3.
- Embodied systems span navigation, agent infrastructure, data synthesis, and dexterous manipulation. ABot-N1 (2607.10383) unifies five navigation tasks; ABot-AgentOS (2607.10350) combines planning, verified skill execution, and persistent memory; Xiaomi-Robotics-U0 (2607.11643) generates robot-centric training observations; and EgoSteer (2607.09701) scales pretraining with curated egocentric video.
- Evidence quality emerges as a distinct evaluation target. AdvancedMathBench (2607.11849) finds that rationale-aware proof verification scores trail binary correctness judgments, while E-VQA (2607.11862) shows that high video-QA accuracy can coexist with weak spatial grounding.
- LightMem-Ego (2607.11487) and ABot-AgentOS (2607.10350) both organize accumulated multimodal experience, but their evidence remains prototype-scale: LightMem-Ego has moderate QA accuracy and high long-term latency, while AgentOS lacks broad real-robot validation.
- Visual-generation work emphasizes controllability: latent facial identity editing (2607.11885), cross-subject motion transfer (2607.11644), and mask-controlled virtual try-on (2607.09362) all seek to preserve appearance while changing selected attributes, trajectories, or garment layout.
- StudioRecon (2607.09125) and LATO.2 (2607.10623) use factorized explicit representations for different purposes—sparse-view human-scene reconstruction and mesh generation—but the supplied evidence does not establish a specific methodological connection between them.
- Several papers expose failure modes rather than only aggregate capability: premature peer commitment in multi-agent systems (2607.11250), fragile and task-dependent metacognition (2607.11881), unresolved conflicts among moral grounds (2607.11736), and response-level rather than pre-generation failure detection in NeuroCogMap (2607.00397).
- The contrastive-learning theory paper (2607.07470) provides an analytical account of sinusoidal filters and partial whitening under restricted assumptions; it should not be generalized directly to modern deep contrastive systems.

## Main results worth noting

- **Relative policy-shift transfer:** Direct-OPD (2607.05394) raises Qwen3-1.7B from 48.3% to 58.3% on AIME 2024 ave@32 and from 36.8% to 43.2% on AIME 2025 ave@32 using the JustRL checkpoint shift. Sequential JustRL then QuestA transfer reaches 63.8% and 46.8%, respectively. PUST (2607.11505), in a different Qwen3 setting, raises an 8B model’s four-benchmark math Mean@16 average from 17.3 to 47.5 using a 4B proxy; its code average rises from 55.9 to 60.5, although the code protocol is inconsistently labeled Mean@8 versus Mean@16.

- **Navigation and embodied execution:** ABot-N1 (2607.10383) reports 77.3% SR and 72.6 SPL on ABotN-POIBench at a 2 m entrance threshold, compared with POINav’s 42.3% and 40.3%. On EVT-Bench, its higher person-following success accompanies 17.9% collision rates in two settings, above ABot-N0’s 11.6% and 7.05%. ABot-AgentOS (2607.10350), on an initial EmbodiedWorldBench subset with the same main and observation models, improves ReAct’s 49.97% TSR/57.95% GCR to 61.96%/68.79%.

- **Scaling robot experience:** Xiaomi-Robotics-U0 (2607.11643) raises average milestone-based task-completion progress under visual interference from 36.9% to 63.2% across three tabletop tasks, with 18 trials per policy–task pair. EgoSteer (2607.09701) reports 30%, 40%, 43%, and 60% mean success on the same ten tasks after scaling egocentric pretraining from none to 3K, 6K, and 9.6K hours; its full system averages 75% across 40 tasks, ten trials each.

- **Persistent memory remains imperfect:** LightMem-Ego (2607.11487) obtains overall retrieval of 51.9 R@1 and 0.627 MRR, but only 55.6% human-judged QA accuracy. Long-term end-to-end P90 latency reaches 35.15 s on phone and 42.70 s on the glasses-style client. AgentOS (2607.10350) reports LoCoMo 87.5 versus reproduced Mem0’s 85.6, but cross-system protocols vary; its controlled static-to-evolving comparisons are smaller, such as LoCoMo 87.5→88.7.

- **Evidence-sensitive evaluation reveals hidden weaknesses:** AdvancedMathBench (2607.11849) reports that moving from binary-polarity to rationale-aware proof evaluation lowers average TNR by 12.3 points and Balanced F1 by 9.0 points. E-VQA (2607.11862) reports InternVL-3.5-8B at 79.04% MCQ answer accuracy but only 27.43% spatial-evidence accuracy, near its 25% random baseline. Its tuned 7B model improves t-mean by 27.2 and J&F by 13.8 over UniPixel-7B, while QA improves only one point.

- **Sparse-view reconstruction:** StudioRecon (2607.09125) improves the eight-scene cumulative pipeline from 18.13/0.567/0.429 to 20.54/0.661/0.273 after dense view synthesis, measured as PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS. Temporal consistency injection lowers Warp-L2 from 0.119 to 0.092 but slightly worsens SSIM and LPIPS, so it is a temporal-quality trade-off rather than a uniform gain.

- **Explicit mesh generation:** LATO.2 (2607.10623) reports CD-L2 0.0407, CD-L1 0.0596, HD 0.0657, and \|NC\| 0.8341 on held-out point-cloud-conditioned assets, compared with the nearest listed LATO baseline at 0.0421, 0.0617, 0.0738, and 0.8262. The supplied evidence does not show whether these relatively small differences are statistically stable.

- **Controlled visual generation:** Latent-Identity Tuning (2607.11885) reports Omni-ID at 0.47 identity consistency, 0.79 edit adherence, and 0.91 prompt adherence on basic edits; its user study records 83%–94% overall preference against each advanced-edit baseline across 250 judgments. Motion4Motion (2607.11644) leads the listed FlexiAct human and animal baselines on TS, MF, TC, and AC, but supplies no uncertainty. CtrlVTON (2607.09362) reaches 0.961 mask IoU on VITON-HD-edit versus the best tested proprietary baseline’s 0.873, while maintaining fidelity scores in the competing range.

- **Multi-agent exploration:** MACE (2607.11250) reaches HotpotQA final trial-and-error mean EM/F1 of 0.3133/0.3941, versus 0.2257/0.2810 for prompted exploration and 0.2733/0.3338 for random selection. Its advantage shrinks in homogeneous pools, consistent with—but not definitive proof of—the authors’ capability-diversity account.

- **Theory and interpretability:** Under stationary-image and shallow-network assumptions, 2607.07470 proves Fourier or sinusoidal optimal structures and partial whitening for its modeled contrastive objectives; experimentally, a CIFAR-10 shallow model with richer augmentations reaches 57.5% KNN accuracy versus 33.5% for random weights. NeuroCogMap (2607.00397) reports hallucination-detection mean AUROC of 0.681 for Gemma2-2B and 0.840 for Gemma2-9B-IT, plus story-listening cortical prediction \(r=0.407\); these are distinct evaluations and should not be compared by raw magnitude.

- **Metacognition and moral reasoning:** The survey (2607.11881) reports mixed, context-dependent metacognitive findings across heterogeneous prior studies; its numerical examples are reviewed claims, not new experiments. MET-D (2607.11736) improves MCLASH macro-F1 by 3.71 points on average across three models and MMoralExceptQA by 4.23, but reduces Gemma3-4B MoCa macro-F1 from 40.03 to 34.97 and does not train reliable ground selection or conflict reconciliation.

## Open research directions

1. **Test policy-shift transfer beyond answer-level competition math** (2607.05394, 2607.11505, 2607.11849). Compare Direct-OPD, PUST, vanilla distillation, and direct RL under matched GPU-hours on complete-proof generation, rationale validity, and error localization—not just final-answer accuracy.

2. **Develop predictors of transferable proxy signals** (2607.05394, 2607.11505). Across many proxy–student pairs, test whether support overlap, log-ratio variance, entropy, or calibration on held-out student rollouts predicts eventual gains and negative transfer.

3. **Compare visual-data sources under one robot-policy protocol** (2607.11643, 2607.09701). Hold architecture, robot demonstrations, and total training tokens constant while varying generated robot views, curated human video, conventional augmentation, and mixtures; measure clean-task success, visual-shift robustness, safety, and compute.

4. **Evaluate modular embodied stacks end to end** (2607.10383, 2607.10350). Integrate a grounded navigation controller as an AgentOS plugin and ablate planning, memory, skill isolation, and verification across simulation and repeated physical trials, reporting latency, collisions, interventions, TSR, and GCR.

5. **Stress-test lifelong multimodal memory** (2607.10350, 2607.11487). Run a multi-user, months-long stream with controlled contradictions, deletion requests, bystander-sensitive content, and delayed feedback; track recall, QA faithfulness, provenance, latency, storage growth, privacy leakage, and regression.

6. **Measure evidence completeness rather than only evidence overlap** (2607.11849, 2607.11862). Collect multiple independently valid proofs or video-evidence sets per question, then test whether model support is sufficient, causally relevant, and equivalent to at least one expert-supported path.

7. **Quantify controllability–identity trade-offs with human-aligned metrics** (2607.11885, 2607.11644, 2607.09362). Sweep edit strength, trajectory corruption, and mask noise while measuring localized leakage, appearance preservation, requested control, artifacts, and blinded user preference.

8. **Make explicit geometry robust to missing or incorrect structure** (2607.09125, 2607.10623). For reconstruction, measure seen/unseen depth and temporal consistency under camera-count and overlap sweeps; for generation, compare one-way factorization with iterative vertex–topology repair using manifold, seam, self-intersection, runtime, and memory metrics.

9. **Remove privileged feedback from peer exploration** (2607.11250). Replace reference-based online rewards with delayed, noisy, preference-based, or self-evaluated feedback, and compare relational MACE with simpler UCB/LinUCB baselines under identical seeds and expanding agent pools.

10. **Validate internal-monitoring claims prospectively** (2607.11881, 2607.00397). Compare confidence, metacognitive measures, and NeuroCogMap-style signals prefix by prefix before an answer completes; evaluate early-error AUROC, calibration, intervention benefit, and robustness across architectures without treating functional correspondence as biological equivalence.
