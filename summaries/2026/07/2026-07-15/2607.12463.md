# Function-Aware Fill-in-the-Middle as Mid-Training for Coding Agent Foundation Models

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-15.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.12463) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12463)


## One-sentence takeaway

Function-aware FIM mid-training on structurally selected Python functions consistently improves post-trained coding agents on SWE-Bench while reducing capability erosion, though evidence beyond Python, Qwen models, and modular code remains limited.

## Problem and motivation

Coding-agent foundation models are generally pretrained with left-to-right prediction or random-span FIM, then adapted using agent trajectories. The authors identify a gap between these stages: pretraining does not explicitly expose the action–observation–continuation conditioning pattern required when agents consume external tool outputs.

They argue that ordinary function calls provide an analogous structure—context, call, externally computed return, and continuation—but random-span FIM weakly captures it because spans are syntactically arbitrary, lack reasoning supervision, and are mixed diffusely into large-scale pretraining (Sections 1 and 2.1).

## Approach and contributions

- **Function-aware target selection:** Build per-file program dependency graphs and select single functions or connected groups of two to three functions using hand-designed complexity, contextual inferability, coupling, and difficulty scores (Section 2.3; Appendix B).

- **Reasoning-augmented FIM:** Train the model to generate a Gemini-3-Flash rationale followed by its predicted implementation inside the FIM middle. The original body is used only to filter candidates, not as the training target (Section 2.4; Appendices B.9–B.11).

- **Dedicated mid-training stage:** Apply one epoch of FIM training to Qwen2.5-Coder-Instruct 7B/14B and Qwen3-8B before otherwise unchanged R2E-Gym, SWE-Smith, or SWE-Lego post-training (Section 3.1; Tables 7–8).

- **Released-artifact proposal:** The paper describes a decontaminated corpus of approximately 400K samples and 2.6B tokens from 968 repositories, plus selection code and checkpoints (Section 1; Table 5). However, the checklist says these artifacts were not available with the submission and were planned for camera-ready release.

The results establish consistent improvements relative to the authors’ reproduced post-training baselines across the tested Qwen configurations. Ablations support contributions from both structured FIM and rationale supervision.

They do not prove that function-call/tool-call structural correspondence is the unique cause of cross-domain gains. Nor do they establish broad cross-family or cross-language generalization. Three-seed standard deviations are reported for Table 1, but no significance tests or uncertainty estimates accompany several secondary and ablation tables.

## Limitations and validity risks

**Author-stated limitations:**

- Training and primary evaluation are Python-only; cross-language evidence is indirect (Section 6).
- The default method depends on the closed Gemini-3-Flash teacher, although self-CoT recovers much of the measured gain (Section 6; Table 3A).
- Only one non-Qwen2.5 configuration is tested, and it simultaneously changes the base model and post-training pipeline (Sections 3.2 and 6).
- Selection assumes modular code and is not systematically tested on monolithic scripts, generated code, or notebooks (Section 6).

**Analyst inferences:**

- The cross-domain results are consistent with the proposed inductive-bias explanation but cannot isolate it from other effects of continued training or teacher-generated supervision.
- The within-file training granularity limits support for cross-file coordination: multi-file Verified tasks show no differential improvement (Appendix D.6).
- Longer behavior carries a substantial inference-cost risk: the method reaches the step cap on about 45% of trajectories versus 7% for the baseline (Appendix D.3).
- Table 3 ablations are restricted to 7B and a 200K-target budget, so their rankings may not fully transfer to the main 400K-sample recipe.
- Artifact availability is ambiguous: the paper presents a public repository link and claims open release, while its checklist says code, data, and new assets were not released with the submission.

## Open research questions

1. **Does the method transfer across programming languages?**  
   This remains open because the corpus and primary agent benchmarks are Python-only. A next experiment could build equivalent Java, C++, and Rust corpora and compare language-specific and multilingual mid-training under the same post-training pipeline.

2. **Can cross-file structural masking improve multi-file issue resolution?**  
   Current masking stays within files, and multi-file Verified tasks receive no differential benefit. A controlled experiment could mask dependency-linked functions across files and report results by gold-patch topology.

3. **How much improvement comes from function-aware structure versus generic continued training?**  
   Random target selection is ablated, but the supplied text does not report a token-matched left-to-right continued-training control. Such a control would better isolate the objective and data-selection effects.

4. **Can recovery gains be retained with lower inference cost?**  
   The stronger agent uses more steps and frequently reaches its cap. An experiment varying step budgets or adding efficiency-aware post-training could measure pass rate against tokens, tool calls, and wall-clock cost.

## Confidence and missing information

**Confidence: high** for the reported method, benchmark results, and stated limitations because the supplied text includes detailed main tables, ablations, algorithms, prompts, and hyperparameters.

Material uncertainties remain about actual artifact availability, the statistical significance of secondary results, per-run training cost, performance outside the Qwen family, non-Python behavior, monolithic or notebook code, and whether cross-domain improvements arise specifically from the proposed structural isomorphism.
