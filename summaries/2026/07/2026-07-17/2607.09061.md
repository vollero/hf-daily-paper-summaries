# On Locality and Length Generalization in Visual Reasoning

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-17.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.09061) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09061)


## One-sentence takeaway

The authors show that strictly local perception combined with recurrent state updates generalizes beyond trained visual task lengths on synthetic state-tracking tasks, whereas global perception or non-recurrent processing tends to learn shortcuts that fail out of distribution.

## Problem and motivation

The paper studies whether visual reasoning models trained on short, relatively simple images can extrapolate to images containing more objects, longer dependencies, or higher resolutions. The authors argue that standard evaluations obscure this capability because most vision models process an entire image globally, permitting shortcuts that perform well in distribution without learning repeatable state-update rules.

The identified gap is that prior length-generalization research largely concerns already-tokenized sequences; visual reasoning additionally requires locating and gathering spatially distributed evidence. The paper therefore tests whether sequential, foveated perception supplies a useful inductive bias for both information gathering and state tracking (Introduction; Sections 2 and 6).

## Approach and contributions

1. **Controlled visual length-generalization test bed:** Three synthetic tasks—Visual Parity, State Machine, and Recall—and a plot-based Finding Roots task vary object counts, image resolution, and reasoning length (Section 2; Figure 2).

2. **FoveAgent-LSTM:** A four-layer LSTM processes a high-resolution local crop and a larger, downsampled peripheral crop at every step. ResNet-18 encoders produce visual features; the model recurrently predicts its next fixation, a stop decision, intermediate probes, and the task output (Sections 3 and D; Figure 3).

3. **Locality and recurrence controls:** The experiments separately vary whether perception is local or global and whether a fixed local observation sequence is processed by strict recurrence, attention, or other sequence architectures (Sections 4.2 and 4.5; Figures 5 and 8).

4. **Active-search extension:** A no-arrow Visual Parity variant adds zoom actions and visible visitation marks, testing state tracking when the model must discover its own traversal path (Section 4.4; Figure 7).

5. **Plot-reasoning adaptation:** FoveAgent-Qwen supplements a coarse global view with predicted \(200\times200\) high-resolution crops and is compared with a fine-tuned global-view Qwen2.5-VL-3B baseline, including compute-matched evaluation (Sections 4.7 and D; Table 1; Figure 10).

For Visual Parity and State Machine, the evaluation counts an FoveAgent-LSTM prediction as incorrect if it fails to visit every relevant object, even when its final class is coincidentally correct (Appendix B). Finding Roots uses relaxed, partial scoring: a root is correct within one-quarter of an x-axis tick interval, and credit is proportional to correctly predicted roots (Appendix B).

The controls support the authors’ claim that both the visual interface and computational backbone affect extrapolation within these tasks. They do not establish that locality and recurrence are universally necessary or sufficient for visual compositional reasoning: the Recall result supplies a counterexample to universal benefit, and the real-world experiment uses an attention-based VLM rather than a strictly recurrent foundation model.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated:** Policies are trained through imitation of ground-truth trajectories. Such policies are straightforward to construct for synthetic tasks, but learning broadly general search policies remains open and may require more varied tasks or reinforcement learning (Section 6).

- **Author-stated:** Finding Roots’ OOD-numroots split is only moderate extrapolation from nearby training counts, not the stronger systematic length generalization tested synthetically (Section 4.7).

- **Author-stated:** FoveAgent-Qwen is used because the authors identify no suitable large pretrained recurrent VLM for mathematical plots. Consequently, the real-world experiment supports foveated evidence gathering but not the claimed necessity of strict recurrence (Sections 4.7 and 6).

- **Analyst inference:** The strongest causal evidence comes from a small collection of constructed tasks—principally parity and a modulo-3 state machine—so generalization to natural images and less structured reasoning is uncertain.

- **Analyst inference:** The no-arrow active-search result depends on zoom controls and black-dot visitation marks that provide external memory; the supplied text does not isolate their individual contributions (Section 4.4).

- **Analyst inference:** Figures 4, 5, 7–9, 11, and 12 lack exact numerical values in the retrieved text, limiting quantitative verification and comparison.

- **Analyst inference:** Dataset sizes and training hardware are documented, but random-seed variance, confidence intervals, and statistical tests are not established in the supplied text.

## Open research questions

1. **Can foveated policies be learned without oracle trajectories?** Oracle imitation limits scalability (Section 6). A next experiment could compare imitation learning, reinforcement learning, and a hybrid curriculum on identical unseen task families.

2. **Do locality and strict recurrence help on less synthetic visual reasoning?** The strongest systematic evidence comes from constructed switch tasks. A next experiment could create natural-image tasks with controlled dependency length while preserving exact state-tracking labels.

3. **How much do external visitation marks enable active search?** The no-arrow experiment introduces marks together with zoom actions, leaving their separate effects unresolved. A factorial ablation could independently remove marks, zooming, and recurrent memory.

4. **Can a pretrained recurrent VLM combine plot understanding with systematic length extrapolation?** FoveAgent-Qwen is not strictly recurrent, and its root-count shift is moderate. A next experiment could train a recurrent multimodal backbone and evaluate it on substantially longer root sequences at matched compute.
