# GRASP: GRanularity-Aware Search Policy for Agentic RAG

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-17.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.10463) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10463)


## One-sentence takeaway

GRASP uses reinforcement learning to coordinate sentence-level semantic and keyword search with selective paragraph expansion, improving retrieval recall and multi-hop QA over the evaluated static, prompted-agent, and RL-agent baselines.

## Problem and motivation

Agentic RAG must decide when to retrieve, which retrieval signal to use, and how much context to expose at each reasoning step. The authors argue that existing systems commonly rely on one retriever or fixed, coarse chunks, making them vulnerable to missed evidence, distracting context, and cascading errors across multi-step trajectories (§1; Figure 1). The identified gap is joint learning of retrieval-signal selection, context granularity, and multi-step tool policy.

## Approach and contributions

- **Granularity-aware tool interface:** GRASP searches a sentence-level corpus using either dense semantic retrieval or BM25-style lexical retrieval, then optionally expands a selected sentence to its parent paragraph (§3.1).
- **RL retrieval policy:** A Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct policy learns through GRPO to interleave reasoning, the three retrieval tools, and answer termination (§§3.3–3.4; Figure 2).
- **Multi-objective trajectory reward:** Token-level answer F1 is combined with rewards for reading gold paragraphs, surfacing gold evidence with both search tools, and finishing efficiently after obtaining a sufficiently correct answer (§3.2).
- **Behavioral analysis:** The authors report an emergent pattern of broad semantic exploration, contextual verification, and entity-focused keyword search (§5.2).
- **Empirical evaluation:** The policy is compared with single-step retrieval, IRCoT, Search-R1, and an untrained base-agent setting on three multi-hop QA benchmarks (§§4–5).

These results establish superiority over the particular reported baselines under the paper’s sampled, corpus-based multi-hop QA setup. They do not establish generalization to arbitrary corpora, other task families, larger backbones, or deployment-scale retrieval. The ablation narrative supports the method’s components qualitatively, but the missing Table 3 values prevent assessing effect sizes.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated:** Grounded-reading and complementary-search rewards require gold supporting-fact annotations, restricting training to specially annotated datasets (Limitations).
- **Author-stated:** The policy sometimes terminates before retrieving all required evidence; final training trajectories stabilized at approximately eight tool calls (Limitations).
- **Author-stated:** Only Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct was tested, so scale-dependent behavior is unknown (Limitations).
- **Analyst inference:** Evaluation uses 500 randomly sampled validation questions per benchmark rather than complete test sets; uncertainty estimates or significance tests are not reported, so the stability of small differences—such as 0.90 versus 0.91 HotpotQA recall—is unclear (§4.1; Table 1).
- **Analyst inference:** GRASP is trained on HotpotQA but its auxiliary rewards directly exploit gold-document identities. Although 2Wiki and MuSiQue results support dataset transfer, the evidence does not isolate transfer of reasoning policy from similarities among benchmark formats.
- **Analyst inference:** The binary JD metric and IRCoT trace generation both use the same proprietary model, gpt5-mini, creating a possible evaluator–baseline dependency that is not analyzed (§§4.3–4.4).
- **Analyst inference:** Exact ablation scores are unavailable in the supplied text, limiting verification of claims about complementary search and granularity (Table 3).
- **Analyst inference:** Code, trained checkpoints, and complete visible hyperparameters are not supplied here; the text gives substantial infrastructure detail but does not establish artifact availability.

## Open research questions

1. **Can GRASP be trained without gold supporting facts?** This remains open because two auxiliary rewards depend on gold evidence. A next experiment could replace them with attribution- or consistency-based rewards and compare QA, recall, and distractor-read rates against fully supervised rewards.

2. **How does policy behavior change with model scale?** Only a 3B backbone is evaluated. Training otherwise matched 1B, 3B, 7B, and 14B policies could measure convergence speed, tool transitions, premature termination, and QA performance.

3. **Can granularity become fully dynamic?** The current interface switches between sentences and whole parent paragraphs. A next experiment could offer span-, sentence-, paragraph-, and document-level actions while measuring accuracy, retrieved-token volume, and latency.

4. **Does complementary-tool coordination remain beneficial in less curated retrieval environments?** Evaluation uses benchmark-derived corpora of supporting and distractor paragraphs. Testing on a substantially larger corpus with controlled distractor density could reveal whether recall gains persist and whether tool costs or error propagation increase.
