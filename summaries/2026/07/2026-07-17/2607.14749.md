# WanSong v1.0 Technical Report

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-17.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.14749) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14749)


## One-sentence takeaway
WanSong v1.0 is presented as a pure diffusion, single-stage text-to-song model that generates up to 5-minute multilingual songs with separate vocal and background-music stems, with reported gains on VAE reconstruction and internal song-generation benchmarks.

## Problem and motivation
The authors address long-form controllable song generation, where they claim existing autoregressive or cascaded AR-plus-diffusion systems can be inefficient and may struggle to maintain perceptual quality over long audio. The identified gap is an end-to-end, non-AR framework that can generate high-fidelity multilingual songs while preserving both vocal accuracy and background-music quality, and while producing editable separated stems.

## Approach and contributions
1. Pure diffusion song-generation framework: audio is represented as continuous VAE tokens and generated with a hybrid-MMDit diffusion backbone conditioned on text captions.

2. Dual-stem modeling: vocals and BGM are modeled as separate output streams, with joint learning inside transformer blocks, to reduce interference between vocal accuracy and accompaniment strength.

3. Training and alignment pipeline: the model uses staged training for 90s, 300s, and SFT phases, plus RLHF using reward models for musicality, lyric accuracy, and prompt alignment.

4. Data and evaluation assets: the paper describes a curated multilingual training set exceeding 6 million hours and evaluates on internal WanSong benchmarks plus SeedTTS for VAE reconstruction.

The evidence supports the authors’ claim that their VAE configuration improves reconstruction on the reported benchmarks and that WanSong is competitive or stronger on selected internal evaluation metrics. It does not establish general performance across independent public song-generation benchmarks, because key evaluations use internal datasets, internal scoring models, and comparisons whose generation settings are not fully specified.

## Limitations and validity risks
Authors state that the SongEval score differences are small and that its generalization is hard to guarantee because the evalscore was trained on nearly 2,000 songs. They also state that expert assessors were used for audio quality in the token-compression ablation because existing evaluators could not precisely measure song audio quality.

Analyst inference: reproducibility is limited by missing details about the 6M-hour dataset composition, data access, exact model training budget for the full generator, reward-model data, human annotation protocol, and whether WanSong weights or benchmarks are released. The strongest song-generation results depend on internal benchmarks and an internal musicality evaluator, so external validity is uncertain. The paper reports a dual-stem design motivation but does not provide a direct ablation isolating dual-stem modeling in the supplied text.

## Open research questions
1. How much of WanSong’s final generation quality comes from dual-stem modeling versus the diffusion backbone and data scale? This remains open because no direct single-stem versus dual-stem ablation is reported; a next experiment would train matched single-stem and dual-stem models on the same data and compare vocal accuracy, BGM quality, and stem separation quality.

2. Does the model generalize beyond the internal WanSong Bench? This remains open because the main song benchmark is internally collected; a next experiment would evaluate on a public or independently curated multilingual song-generation benchmark with fixed prompts and blind human ratings.

3. What is the impact of RLHF on musicality, lyric accuracy, and prompt alignment separately? This remains open because the supplied text describes DPO and ReFL but does not report pre/post-RLHF ablations; a next experiment would compare the base, DPO-only, and DPO-plus-ReFL models across the three reward dimensions and human preference tests.

4. What inference speed and compute cost are achieved after step distillation? This remains open because the abstract claims faster inference but no latency, step count, hardware, or quality-speed tradeoff table is provided; a next experiment would measure generation time and quality across diffusion step counts.
