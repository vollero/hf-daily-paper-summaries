# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-16

## Executive summary

- Agent systems dominate the day: papers address mobile execution, evaluation infrastructure, trajectory diagnosis, repository understanding, compiler feedback, self-improvement, and security-agent assessment.
- Two mobile-agent designs expose a useful architectural contrast. KnowAct-GUIClaw (2607.12625) combines host routing, GUI execution, memory, and reusable skills; PalmClaw (2607.13027) favors Android-native orchestration and permission-checked device tools.
- Agent diagnosis advances along complementary lines: Oat (2607.12747) learns deviations from successful trajectories, whereas STRACE (2607.07702) backward-slices explicit execution-dependency graphs.
- Reasoning efficiency carries an oversight risk. Ring-Zero (2607.12395) reports adaptive, shorter mathematical reasoning, while 2607.09786 finds that length-penalized RL can preserve much answer accuracy yet make answer-influencing hints less visible.
- Visual-generation work spans unified image generation/editing, pixel-space diffusion registers, monocular novel-view synthesis, and 3D/4D consistency correction. Several comparisons are promising but cannot be quantified because supplied tables are incomplete.
- OvisOCR2 (2607.13639) reports strong document-parsing scores from a compact 0.8B model, although degraded real captures remain harder than clean or digital documents.
- PolicyShiftGuard (2607.05910) shows why ordinary moderation F1 is insufficient for runtime-changing policies: existing guardrails can score reasonably on F1 while failing paired same-image policy flips.
- Embodied work ranges from a fast real-robot action policy (2607.13960) and a programmable simulator (2607.06701) to UAV self-awareness evaluation (2607.12477) and proactive egocentric assistance (2607.11523).
- Several results rely on narrow platforms, author-built benchmarks, LLM judges, limited real-world trials, or validation-only tuning. These settings should be retained when interpreting reported gains.

## Main results worth noting

- **Repository planning — 2607.13285:** On 30 requests per harness, Handbook-assisted planning improved judged win rate from 28.3% to 38.3% for Codex and from 26.7% to 45.6% for Terminus-2, while reducing planner tokens per request from 0.102M to 0.089M and 0.058M to 0.053M. These are plan-quality results, not patch-correctness results.

- **Streaming Rust feedback — 2607.13921:** Across seven models, two Rust task families, and two samples per model-task, Generative Compilation reduced average compiler-error rate from 20.7% under post-generation compilation to 13.1%. It achieved the highest unit-test correctness in 11 of 14 configurations and reduced average feedback overhead from 233 to 135 seconds over direct generation.

- **Mobile execution — 2607.12625 and 2607.13027:** KnowAct-GUIClaw reached 64.1% SR/pass@1 on 117 MobileWorld GUI-only tasks with a 50-step cap. PalmClaw reached 97.1% success on its separate 70-task MobileTask suite, averaging 17.7 seconds and 2.8 actions. The scores should not be compared as a leaderboard because the benchmarks, platforms, and action spaces differ.

- **Agent failure diagnosis — 2607.12747 and 2607.07702:** Oat achieved MCP-Atlas F1 \(0.435\pm0.013\), versus 0.212 for GPT-4o and 0.181 for GPT-5 prompting, and Who&When top-k F1 \(0.225\pm0.003\). STRACE raised VeruSAGE-Bench success from 42.5% to 58.5%; removing structural modeling in its reported ablation lowered success from 56% to 48% and increased optimizer-call cost from 2.9563 to 5.1045.

- **Evaluation infrastructure and protocol — 2607.13705 and 2605.10834:** AgentCompass reports that harness/configuration choices can move scores materially, including cited gaps of −8.7 points on DeepSearchQA and +15.0 on SWE-bench-Pro relative to official baselines. The pentesting protocol’s three-run cumulative evaluation improved F1 for all tested configurations mainly through recall, showing why single-run success misses stochastic discovery behavior.

- **Zero-RL scaling — 2607.12395:** Under pass@1 aggregated over 64 runs, first-stage 1T vs 104B results included 89.1 vs 71.2 on AIME 2024, 84.2 vs 65.3 on AIME 2026, and 66.2 vs 50.3 on HMMT February 2026. Second-stage 1T with Yarn=2 reached 93.2 on AIME 2026, 81.0 on HMMT February 2026, and 75.5 on IMOAnswerBench.

- **Reasoning-monitorability trade-off — 2607.09786:** At the strongest length target, Qwen3-14B MMLU-Pro-R accuracy changed from 78.2% to 75.3%, while median CoT length and monitor catch rate changed from 878 tokens/69% to 330/49%. Random-deletion controls placed the additional disclosure loss beyond length alone at 7.3–34.7 percentage points across evaluated cells.

- **Pruning recovery — 2607.13124:** For a roughly 25%-pruned Qwen3-4B-Instruct, average free-form score rose from 5.71 to 48.46 after one ShortOPD epoch and 55.41 after three, against 75.17 for the dense teacher. Dynamic scheduling used 250M rollout tokens and 8.5 hours versus 869M and 35.9 hours for fixed-8192 OPD, with schedule averages ranging from 48.5 to 50.2.

- **Policy adaptation — 2607.05910:** PolicyShiftGuard-7B scored 76.9 average F1 and 72.1 paired Policy Shift Score, compared with 20.6 and 4.8 for Qwen2.5-VL-7B. Removing the pair loss reduced 7B average F1 from 76.9 to 59.2, supporting the specific value of same-image boundary training.

- **Document parsing — 2607.13639:** The 0.8B OvisOCR2 scored 96.58 overall on OmniDocBench v1.6 versus 96.33 for PaddleOCR-VL-1.6 and 94.74 for HunyuanOCR-1.5. On PureDocBench, its 75.06 Avg3 comprised 81.55 Clean, 77.09 Digital, and 66.56 Real; the Real score trailed Gemini-3.1-Pro’s 71.98.

- **Image generation — 2607.13125 and 2605.16147:** In Boogu’s reported evaluation, Base-Thinking scored 53.57 Chinese and 53.73 English overall on Qwen-Image-Bench, below GPT-Image-2 at 64.69/65.23 but highest among evaluated open systems. Separately, registers improved ImageNet-256 FID for pDiT-B/16 from 4.80 to 3.80 and H/16 from 2.35 to 2.02 at 600 epochs; these are not comparable with Boogu’s instruction-following scores.

- **3D/4D generation — 2607.12000 and 2607.12752:** Both papers report directional gains and ablations supporting geometry or consistency interventions, but the supplied MetaView and Hallo4D analyses omit their central numerical tables. Their result magnitudes and metric-wise rankings therefore cannot be verified here.

- **Robot policy and simulation — 2607.13960 and 2607.06701:** GigaWorld-Policy-0.5 reported average real-robot SR of 0.85 for fruit picking, 0.89 for placement, and 0.77 for long-horizon tasks, with ten trials per task; its C++ path reached 85 ms on RTX 4090. SPEAR delivered 1920×1080 images at 73.4 FPS with shared memory and two-frame latency on its reported workstation, versus 3.5 FPS for UnrealCV+ in a controlled but non-identical-scene comparison.

- **Embodied video — 2607.11523 and 2607.12477:** EgoMemo improved EgoServe overall F1 to 8.0 from 4.7 for GPT-5-mini, but its response-quality score was 2.8 versus 3.0. On SIS-Bench, humans scored 91.7% versus 71.6% for the best model; motion fusion improved spatial/self averages from 72.0/60.3 to 74.2/63.7, while Path Planning remained weak at 23.5% versus a 25.2% random baseline.

- **Affect estimation — 2607.13250:** On the local s-Aff-Wild2 validation split, low-rate DINOv3 fine-tuning raised the composite from 0.831 to 1.045, and validation-tuned AU/expression calibration produced a final 1.177. Fear F1 increased from 0.038 to 0.331, but no untouched test result establishes that the calibration generalizes.

- **Survey contributions — 2607.13104 and 2607.13431:** These papers contribute organizing frameworks rather than new benchmark victories: parameter-versus-scaffold updates for agent self-improvement, and a tokenization-aware four-component decomposition for discrete diffusion. Both supplied sources are truncated.

## Open research directions

1. **Evaluate complete software changes, not only intermediate artifacts (2607.13285, 2607.13921).** Execute handbook-derived plans and compiler-guided generations on the same repository tasks, then measure test pass rate, semantic regressions, human-review findings, latency, and cost.

2. **Separate mobile action-space effects from model and benchmark effects (2607.12625, 2607.13027).** Run GUI-only, shortcut-enabled, and structured-device-tool variants with the same backbone, phone fleet, tasks, permissions, and judging procedure.

3. **Distinguish anomalous steps from causal root causes (2607.12747, 2607.07702, 2607.13705).** Build an independently annotated trajectory set with anomaly, manifestation, and root-cause labels; compare Oat, STRACE, and combined analyzers using localization precision/recall and downstream repair success.

4. **Jointly measure reasoning efficiency and oversight (2607.12395, 2607.09786).** Apply paired hint interventions and truncation-null controls to Ring-Zero’s distilled and Low/Medium/High tiers while retaining pass@1, correct-trace tokens, and judge-independent disclosure measures.

5. **Test visual-generation interventions outside their original regimes (2605.16147, 2607.13125, 2607.12000, 2607.12752).** Evaluate register training/guidance in a text-conditioned DiT and geometry/consistency interventions under shared cross-view prompts, reporting instruction fidelity, perceptual quality, spatial consistency, runtime, and human preference separately.

6. **Stress policy adaptation beyond a fixed English catalog (2607.05910).** Construct same-content policy-flip cases using long informal rules, legal-style policies, multilingual policies, unseen jurisdictions, video, and multi-turn context; retain paired PSS alongside ordinary F1.

7. **Establish recovery boundaries after compression (2607.13124).** Sweep pruning operator, compression ratio, model family, and scale under matched recovery compute, measuring the point at which ShortOPD can no longer restore coherent generation.

8. **Move embodied evaluations toward deployment realism (2607.13960, 2607.06701, 2607.12477, 2607.11523).** Combine controlled simulation with multi-robot or closed-loop trials, longer temporal histories, sensor perturbations, latency distributions, and intervention-preference labels.

9. **Validate author-built metrics and calibrations externally (2607.13639, 2607.12000, 2607.13250).** Use an independently curated degraded-document set, blinded human validation of Dense Matching Distance, and untouched affect test data with uncertainty-calibration metrics.

10. **Evaluate durable self-improvement over repeated cycles (2607.13104, 2607.13431).** For agent scaffolds, measure improvement curves, rollback, regressions, memory poisoning, and safety violations; for discrete diffusion, test whether proposed tokenizer diagnostics predict quality under matched end-to-end training.
