# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-16

## Executive summary
- Agent infrastructure is the day’s strongest theme: 2607.13285, 2607.13705, 2607.12747, and 2607.07702 all focus on making agent behavior easier to evaluate, localize, debug, or improve from traces and harness structure.
- Mobile and personal assistants split into GUI-memory systems and native device-tool systems: 2607.12625 reports 64.1% MobileWorld SR in its best setting, while 2607.13027 reports 97.1% success on its 70-task MobileTask setup.
- Safety evaluation work emphasizes behavior under changed conditions rather than ordinary accuracy: 2607.05910 tests same-image policy flips, and 2607.09786 shows length-penalized CoT compression can preserve answer behavior while reducing monitor catch rate.
- Multimodal generation papers target controllability, consistency, and model internals: 2607.13125 covers open-source image generation/editing, 2607.12000 monocular novel view synthesis, 2605.16147 register tokens in pixel-space DiTs, 2607.12752 3D/4D hallucination mitigation, and 2607.13431 a discrete-diffusion survey.
- Several reports are strong on method design but weaker on auditable effect sizes because supplied text omitted numeric tables, especially 2607.12000, 2607.12752, 2607.13705, 2607.13431, and parts of 2607.07702.
- Document and structured visual understanding are active: 2607.13639 reports 96.58 OmniDocBench v1.6 overall with a 0.8B parser, while 2607.12477 shows a >20-point human-model gap on UAV self-in-space video QA.
- Embodied AI contributions span real robots and simulators: 2607.13960 reports 0.85 fruit-picking SR and 85 ms RTX 4090 C++ latency, while 2607.06701 reports 73.4 FPS 1080p image delivery with shared memory and two-frame latency.
- Training and compression work includes trillion-parameter zero RL for math reasoning in 2607.12395 and short-to-long on-policy distillation for pruned LLM recovery in 2607.13124.

## Main results worth noting
- 2607.13285: On two agent harnesses and 60 modification requests, Handbook-assisted planning improved author-reported win rate on Codex from 28.3% to 38.3% and on Terminus-2 from 26.7% to 45.6%, while reducing planner tokens by 12.7% and 8.6%.
- 2607.13125: In Boogu Arena, Boogu-Image-0.1-Turbo-Thinking reached Elo 1048 among listed models, below GPT-Image-2 1196 and Nano-Banana-Pro 1087 but above several evaluated open-source systems; on ImgEdit-Bench, Boogu-Image-0.1-Edit-Thinking reports 4.64 overall.
- 2607.12395: Ring-2.5-1T-Zero First Stage exceeded the 104B First Stage on all shared reported math benchmarks; for AIME 2026, scores were 84.2 vs 65.3, and for HMMT Feb 2026, 66.2 vs 50.3.
- 2607.13639: OvisOCR2 reports 96.58 overall on OmniDocBench v1.6 and 75.06 Avg3 on PureDocBench, but its PureDocBench Real score 66.56 trails Gemini-3.1-Pro 71.98 and Qwen3.5-122B-A10B 69.85.
- 2607.05910: PolicyShiftGuard-7B reports 76.9 Avg F1 and 72.1 Avg PSS on POLICYSHIFTBENCH, compared with Qwen2.5-VL-7B at 20.6 Avg F1 and 4.8 Avg PSS.
- 2605.16147: Register tokens improved pDiT FID on ImageNet 256 at 600 epochs: B/16 from 4.80 to 3.80, L/16 from 2.80 to 2.47, and H/16 from 2.35 to 2.02.
- 2607.13124: ShortOPD improved a 25%-pruned Qwen3-4B-Instruct generation average from 5.71 to 48.46 after one epoch; three epochs reached 55.41, reported as 73.7% of the dense teacher.
- 2607.12747: Oat reports MCP-Atlas F1 `0.435 ± 0.013` with conformal prediction and Who&When top-k F1 `0.225 ± 0.003`, outperforming GPT-4o and GPT-5 prompting baselines in those settings.
- 2607.09786: Under 30% length target, Qwen3-14B MMLU-Pro-R accuracy drops from 78.2% to 75.3%, while monitor catch rate drops from 69% at 878 median tokens to 49% at 330 tokens.
- 2607.12477: SIS-Bench reports a human overall score of 91.7% versus best model 71.6%; SIS-Motion improves Spatial Avg 72.0 to 74.2 and Self Avg 60.3 to 63.7 over visual-only SFT.
- 2607.13960: GigaWorld-Policy-0.5 reports 0.85 fruit-picking SR, 0.89 object-placement SR, and 0.77 long-horizon SR, with C++ deployment at 85 ms on RTX 4090 and SR 0.85.
- 2607.06701: SPEAR reports 73.4 FPS for 1920×1080 image delivery with shared memory and two-frame latency, compared with UnrealCV+ at 3.5 FPS in the controlled rendering benchmark.
- 2607.13250: AffectFlow-DINO’s best validation result is `P_MTL=1.177`; the paper attributes the largest single gain to DINOv3 fine-tuning and substantial gains to per-AU and per-expression calibration.

## Open research directions
1. Agent localization to executed fixes: motivated by 2607.13285, 2607.07702, and 2607.12747. Run matched experiments where localized plans or root-cause slices are executed into patches or prompt updates, then evaluate final task success and regression rate rather than plan quality or attribution F1 alone.
2. Standardized trajectory diagnostics: motivated by 2607.13705 and 2607.12747. Build a manually labeled trajectory-failure corpus and measure analyzer precision/recall for loops, reward-hacking suspicion, truncation, and failure-contributing steps.
3. Durable mobile-agent memory and skill drift: motivated by 2607.12625 and 2607.13027. Re-run stored GUI skills and device-tool workflows across app updates, OS versions, and permission changes, measuring repair success, unsafe boundary violations, and task success.
4. Safety behavior under conditional shifts: motivated by 2607.05910 and 2607.09786. Extend paired policy/hint interventions to video, multimodal dialogue, and agentic tasks, while measuring both final behavior and disclosure or auditability.
5. Quantitative consistency metrics for generative spatial systems: motivated by 2607.12000 and 2607.12752. Compare DMD, CLIP-style scores, dense correspondence, temporal consistency, and human ratings on the same NVS/3D/4D outputs.
6. On-policy distillation boundaries: motivated by 2607.13639 and 2607.13124. Test OPD across model sizes, compression levels, and modalities with ablations for teacher size, rollout horizon, prompt-domain coverage, and reward or label quality.
7. Simulation-to-real embodied evaluation: motivated by 2607.06701, 2607.13960, and 2607.12477. Generate controlled UAV or manipulation scenarios in simulation, then measure whether simulator-trained or simulator-tested policies transfer to real videos or robots.
8. Calibration versus genuine uncertainty: motivated by 2607.13250. Evaluate flow-sampled uncertainty on held-out data with calibration curves, ambiguity labels, and error prediction, separating validation-tuned threshold gains from probabilistic uncertainty quality.
9. Realistic security-agent evaluation at scale: motivated by 2605.10834. Expand finding-level pentesting evaluation to private or newly built targets, add destructive-action safety metrics, and test cross-run memory against independent expert adjudication.
10. Discrete diffusion design validation: motivated by 2607.13431 and 2605.16147. Train matched discrete-diffusion systems across tokenizers and corruption kernels, then test whether proposed tokenizer diagnostics predict downstream generation quality.
