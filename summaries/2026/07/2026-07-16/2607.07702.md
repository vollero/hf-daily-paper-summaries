# From Noisy Traces to Root Causes: Structural Trajectory Analysis and Causal Extraction for Agent Optimization

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-16.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.07702) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07702)


## One-sentence takeaway
STRACE improves long-horizon LLM agents by filtering redundant failure traces and localizing upstream root-cause modules before synthesizing targeted prompt-policy updates.

## Problem and motivation
The paper addresses optimization of long-horizon agent systems from execution traces. The authors argue that trace collections are large, redundant, heterogeneous, and expensive to optimize over directly, while individual traces contain many irrelevant steps that can mislead reflection-based optimizers. They identify a gap in existing prompt-optimization and reflexive-agent methods: full trajectories dilute causal evidence, while truncation or local context can drop distant but important upstream causes, leading optimizers to repair symptoms rather than root causes. Evidence: Sections 1, 2.1, 2.2, Figure 1.

## Approach and contributions
STRACE is a four-phase framework for constructing higher signal-to-noise optimization contexts.

1. **Structural modeling:** builds a textual Execution Dependency Graph from code, prompts, configurations, tool interfaces, and logs, capturing data and control dependencies among modules. Evidence: Sections 3.1, 3.2, Appendix B.2.
2. **Failure pattern mining and trace filtering:** parses batches of traces, summarizes global outcomes, local node status, severity, and structural path patterns, then selects representative failure exemplars. Evidence: Section 3.3, Figure 5.
3. **Causal localization:** performs backward slicing over the dependency graph to keep only causally relevant steps and distinguish manifestation nodes from root-cause nodes. Evidence: Section 3.4, Figure 4.
4. **Inductive policy optimization:** groups localized failures by root-cause module and injects generalized natural-language heuristics into the relevant module prompts. Evidence: Section 3.5, Appendix D.

## Limitations and validity risks
The authors state that STRACE assumes sufficient visibility into the target agent system, including codebase-level or harness-level artifacts such as agent definitions, tool interfaces, configuration files, prompts, skills, and logs. They explicitly say trace-only or fully black-box settings remain future work. Evidence: Limitations.

They also note deployment risks: real execution traces may contain sensitive information, and optimized prompts or harness components should be reviewed before use in high-stakes or user-facing systems. Evidence: Ethical considerations.

Analyst inference: reproducibility is only partially supported by the supplied text. Analyst inference: causal claims depend on the quality of the textual dependency graph and LLM-based causal deduction. The perturbation study suggests robustness to some graph noise, but the supplied text does not show all graph-quality review details or per-model table values.

## Open research questions
1. Can STRACE work in trace-only or black-box agent settings? This remains open because the authors state that sufficient system visibility is required. A plausible next experiment is to replace repository-derived EDGs with graphs inferred only from traces and compare localization accuracy and downstream SR.

2. How accurate are the identified root-cause modules against human labels? The paper gives a case study and expert-reviewed graph discussion, but the supplied text does not report a systematic root-cause-label benchmark. A next experiment would annotate manifestation/root-cause pairs and measure localization precision and recall.

3. How much do gains depend on the optimizer model used for STRACE phases? Appendix B.2 discusses different models for graph construction, but the supplied text does not establish full end-to-end optimizer-model sensitivity. A next experiment would run the same STRACE pipeline with multiple optimizer LLMs and report cost, localization quality, and task SR.

4. Does STRACE generalize to production traces with privacy filtering or incomplete logs? The paper warns that deployment traces may contain sensitive information and assumes sufficient logs. A next experiment would evaluate STRACE after anonymization, redaction, or deliberate log-field removal.
