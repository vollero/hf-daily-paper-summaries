# OvisOCR2 Technical Report

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-16.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.13639) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13639)


## One-sentence takeaway
OvisOCR2 is a compact 0.8B end-to-end document-image-to-Markdown parser that the authors claim surpasses pipeline and end-to-end systems on OmniDocBench v1.6, PureDocBench, and their in-house benchmark.

## Problem and motivation
The paper addresses page-level document parsing: converting visually rich document page images into structured Markdown while preserving text, formulas, tables, visual regions, layout, and reading order. The authors argue this matters because parsed Markdown supports indexing, retrieval, and downstream document workflows. They identify a gap between deployable end-to-end parsers and stronger but more complex pipeline systems: pipeline methods lead public leaderboards but require multiple components and can accumulate unrecoverable stage errors, while existing end-to-end methods simplify deployment but lag in performance (Introduction).

## Approach and contributions
1. **Compact end-to-end parser:** OvisOCR2 post-trains a Qwen3.5-0.8B backbone to generate page-level Markdown directly from a document image in one pass (Introduction, Section 3).

2. **Two-source data engine:** The authors combine filtered real-document annotations with synthetic pages whose rendered images and Markdown labels come from the same HTML source, intended to balance realism with clean long-tail supervision (Section 2, Fig. 2).

3. **Training recipe:** The method uses supervised fine-tuning, GRPO-based RL on a 4B branch with text/formula/table rewards, on-policy distillation into the 0.8B model, and weighted model fusion (Section 3, Fig. 3, Table 1).

4. **Evaluation claim:** The authors report state-of-the-art overall scores on OmniDocBench v1.6 and PureDocBench, plus best overall performance on an in-house benchmark (Section 4, Tables 2–7).

The evidence establishes that, under the reported benchmark protocols and listed comparison sets, OvisOCR2 has the best overall scores on several document parsing evaluations. It does not establish independent reproducibility, because the supplied text does not include full dataset construction details, training data sizes, compute budget, exact hyperparameters, or released evaluation scripts for the in-house benchmark.

## Limitations and validity risks
The authors explicitly note that robustness to degraded real-world images remains an important future direction, citing weaker PureDocBench Real performance relative to strong general VLMs (Section 4.2; Conclusion). They also state future work should further improve handwriting-heavy and complex-table documents (Conclusion).

Analyst inference: reproducibility is limited by missing training corpus sizes, detailed data mixture proportions, RL hyperparameters, OPD top-k value, model fusion weights, and in-house benchmark release details. The in-house benchmark supports additional claims about long-tail scenarios, but its construction and annotation process are described only at a high level, so external validity is hard to assess from the supplied text.

## Open research questions
1. **How much does each training stage contribute?**  
   This remains open because the paper reports the final system but does not provide a full ablation of SFT, RL, OPD, and model fusion. A next experiment would evaluate checkpoints after each stage on OmniDocBench, PureDocBench, and the in-house subsets.

2. **Which parts of the data engine matter most?**  
   The paper describes real-data filtering and HTML-based synthetic generation, but does not quantify their separate effects. A next experiment would compare real-only, synthetic-only, and mixed-data training with matched compute.

3. **Can the model close the gap on degraded real captures?**  
   OvisOCR2 trails some general VLMs on PureDocBench Real. A next experiment would add targeted degradation and recapture augmentation, then measure Clean/Digital/Real tradeoffs.

4. **How robust is OvisOCR2 outside the in-house distribution?**  
   The in-house benchmark is not fully specified in the supplied text. A next experiment would evaluate on a separately curated, externally released benchmark with documented sampling and annotation procedures.

## Confidence and missing information
Confidence: **high** for summarizing the authors’ stated method and reported numbers; **medium** for broader interpretation because several experimental details are absent.

Missing information includes training data sizes, data mixture ratios, compute budget, complete hyperparameters, OPD top-k, model fusion weights, direct ablation tables, in-house benchmark annotation protocol, and whether all evaluation code and benchmark data are publicly available.
