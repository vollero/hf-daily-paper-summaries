# Registers Matter for Pixel-Space Diffusion Transformers

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-16.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.16147) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16147)


## One-sentence takeaway

The authors claim that register tokens improve pixel-space Diffusion Transformers not by removing ViT-style patch-token outliers, but by creating dedicated high-norm/global-information slots that smooth internal feature maps and enable a new Register Guidance sampling method.

## Problem and motivation

The paper asks whether register tokens, previously used to mitigate high-norm token artifacts in ViTs, are useful in Diffusion Transformers as image diffusion models increasingly use transformer backbones and pixel-space training. The gap is that prior register-token explanations are tied to ViT patch-token outliers, while the authors find DiTs do not show the same patch-token outliers, leaving the role of registers in DiTs underexplored.

## Approach and contributions

1. The authors analyze pixel-space and latent-space DiTs with and without appended learnable register tokens, measuring attention maps, token norms, feature smoothness, probing accuracy, and generation quality.
2. They show that DiTs lack ViT-like patch-token outliers but still form high-norm register tokens when registers are added.
3. They argue registers are especially useful in pixel-space DiTs because they reduce patch-token norms and produce smoother intermediate features at high-noise diffusion timesteps.
4. They identify register specialization: some tokens act as norm sinks, while others carry global semantic information.
5. They propose Register Guidance, which uses predictions with and without registers as a guidance direction and combines it with CFG.

The evidence supports the authors’ central empirical claim that registers help pixel-space DiTs and that RG can improve JiT-style models under the reported ImageNet settings. It does not fully establish a causal mechanism for why registers help, and the strongest results are concentrated on ImageNet and JiT/pDiT-like setups.

## Limitations and validity risks

The authors state that the study focuses mainly on ImageNet DiTs and a set of pretrained text-to-image models, so broader datasets and training recipes are not established. They also state that they do not provide a comprehensive explanation of why registers help. Register Guidance adds hyperparameters, including register-drop probability and guidance scale, and RG+CFG requires extra model evaluations, reducing sampling speed versus the original model.

Analyst inference: several mechanistic claims are supported by correlations between norms, probing, TV, and FID, but the supplied text does not show interventions that isolate each proposed role of registers. Text-to-image evidence is observational only because the authors analyze pretrained models rather than training text-to-image models with explicit registers.

## Open research questions

1. Do register tokens improve pixel-space DiTs beyond ImageNet?
This remains open because Appendix E says broader datasets and recipes were not evaluated. A next experiment would train pDiT/JiT variants with and without registers on another image-generation dataset and compare FID/IS plus feature-smoothness metrics.

2. What mechanism causally links register tokens to better generation?
The paper shows smoother features and semantic/norm-sink specialization, but says it lacks a comprehensive explanation. A next experiment would ablate or freeze specific register subsets identified as sinks versus semantic carriers and measure generation quality and feature TV.

3. Can Register Guidance help text-to-image DiTs?
The authors identify text-token outlier behavior in pretrained text-to-image models but leave RG extension to text-to-image as future work. A next experiment would train or fine-tune a text-to-image DiT with register-drop support and compare CFG versus RG+CFG.

4. What is the best architecture for separate register and patch processing?
The paper explores compact dual-stream designs and finds improvements, but Section 4 says dual-stream processing merits further study. A next experiment would scale the compact dual-stream design to larger models and compare parameter/runtime tradeoffs against single-stream JiT.

## Confidence and missing information

Confidence: high.

Material missing information: full training cost and hardware, exact sampling/evaluation protocols beyond the provided hyperparameter tables, statistical variance or confidence intervals for FID/IS, broader non-ImageNet datasets, text-to-image training with explicit registers or RG, and complete causal ablations separating norm-sink and semantic-register roles.
