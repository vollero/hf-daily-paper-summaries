# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-21

## Executive summary

- Video research spans personalized generation, persistent memory, temporal grounding, and synthetic driving data: HOMIE (`2607.18217`), ReflectWorld-MM (`2607.09759`), TimeLens2 (`2607.17423`), and OpenLongTail (`2607.09655`).
- Two reasoning post-training methods retain group-relative RL but add different signals: group entropy in GEPO (`2607.16850`) and teacher token preferences in Distilled RL (`2607.17247`).
- Fine-grained language supervision also appears outside conventional RL: experiential textual coaching (`2607.18110`) and token-local faithful/corrupted labels (`2607.17524`).
- Embodied work ranges from RynnBrain’s cross-embodiment VLA model (`2607.17977`) to JoyNexus’s shared post-training services (`2607.16074`) and OpenLongTail’s generated driving-policy data (`2607.09655`).
- Agent infrastructure is unusually prominent: ESAT synthesizes API trajectories without executable environments (`2607.16900`), FlashRT searches multimodal deployments (`2607.18171`), and SWE-Pruner Pro compresses coding-agent tool context (`2607.18213`).
- EvolvingWorld (`2607.17250`) and ReflectWorld-MM (`2607.09759`) independently support explicit, revisable state outside the immediate context, in literary simulation and audiovisual memory respectively.
- DiffGI (`2607.13365`) targets thin-shell 3D generation with a continuous geometry image, while DiFA (`2607.17972`) improves few-step diffusion sampling without additional neural-network evaluations.
- Reported gains are often substantial, but many studies rely on self-curated or model-generated data, model judges, best-checkpoint selection, limited trials, or missing uncertainty estimates.

## Main results worth noting

- `2607.16900`: On AppWorld Test-N, Qwen3.5-9B trained with ESAT-S52-AW7 achieved 75.7% task-goal completion versus 25.2% zero-shot, a 50.5-point gain averaged over eight inference runs. On OfficeBench 2-app tasks, Qwen3.5-2B improved from 9.1% to 69.6% pass rate.
- `2607.09655`: Across 53 AlpaSim long-tail events with two rollouts per policy/event, Alpamayo-R1 improved from 0.534 to 0.748 average score after synthetic-data SFT, while collision rate changed from 58.8% to 0.0%. Ground-truth multi-view SFT scored 0.764 with 0.0% collisions.
- `2607.17977`: With matched demonstrations, 60K post-training steps, batch size 32, and 20 trials per task, RynnBrain initialization achieved 91.28% process score and 86.67% success versus Qwen initialization’s 68.33% and 60.00%.
- `2607.18171`: On NVIDIA B200, FlashRT reduced a conversational multimodal pipeline’s latency from 107.92 seconds on the sequential baseline to 1.57 seconds using three GPUs. This is a resource-changing deployment comparison, not a same-hardware algorithmic speedup.
- `2607.17972`: With DPM-Solver++ on CIFAR-10, DiFA reduced FID from 8.40 to 4.15 at 8 NFE, from 3.70 to 2.18 at 12 NFE, and from 2.33 to 1.96 at 20 NFE, without extra denoiser evaluations.
- `2607.17423`: TimeLens2’s 2B, 4B, and 8B variants averaged 44.5, 47.7, and 48.0 mIoU over seven temporal-grounding benchmarks, respectively. Under fixed RL settings, adding the temporal Wasserstein reward raised average mIoU from 47.0 to 47.7 and reduced constant-reward rollout groups from 13.8% to 3.6%.
- `2607.09759`: ReflectWorld-MM reported 76.9% VideoMME-L, 69.4% LVBench, and 46.8% EgoLife-QA accuracy, with source-video fallback rates of 6.8% and 4.6% on the reported VideoMME-L and EgoLife-QA efficiency evaluations.
- `2607.16850`: Using each method’s best checkpoint over thirteen benchmarks, GEPO averaged 54.2 versus GRPO’s 51.5 on Intern-S1-mini, and 71.2 versus 70.7 on Qwen3.5-9B. No seed-level uncertainty or matched tuning budget was reported.
- `2607.17247`: Over ten mathematical benchmarks, cross-family DSQW-1.5B with Distilled RL averaged 40.00 Pass@1 versus 35.27 for OPD, 36.86 for RL, and 36.54 for OPD+RL. Removing the negative-reset rule lowered the average by 6.39 points.
- `2607.18110`: For OLMo-3-7B with self-feedback, experiential learning versus matched rubric RL scored 50.8% versus 45.9% on AlpacaEval, 47.5 versus 42.1 on WildBench, and 26.2% versus 23.3% on ArenaHard; CreativeWritingV3 tied at 78.8.
- `2607.18217`: On HOMIE’s 200-sample benchmark, Wan2.2 achieved 0.696 GMEScore and Wan2.1 achieved 0.891 object similarity and 0.452 OCR accuracy. Phantom remained better on Face-Sim, 0.801 versus HOMIE’s 0.786/0.760.
- `2607.13365`: On GarmageSet reconstruction without normal loss, replacing binary occupancy with TSDF reduced Chamfer distance from \(1.503\times10^{-3}\) to \(0.595\times10^{-3}\); adding normal supervision further reduced it to \(0.461\times10^{-3}\) and raised normal consistency from 0.921 to 0.961.
- `2607.16074`: In a matched mixed workload with three RL tenants and one SFT tenant on eight GPUs, JoyNexus reduced aggregate GPU time by 28.3%, corresponding to 1.39× GPU-time efficiency. Final policy equivalence was not evaluated.
- `2607.18213`: On SWE-QA-Pro, SWE-Pruner Pro reduced Qwen3-Coder-Next token use from 607K to 368K while its judge score changed from 7.60 to 7.84. On SWE-Bench Verified, however, the same backbone resolved 335/500 versus 341/500 unpruned.
- `2607.17250`: On the same EvolvingWorld test set, GPT-5.3-Chat’s character average was 85.52 versus BookWorld’s 70.64. Removing character-state updates reduced it to 69.73; removing both character and world-state updates reduced it to 66.78.
- `2607.17524`: The authors report that TOPL produced the best average OOD summarization factuality across the evaluated three backbones and the highest compared OOD translation XCOMET, but the supplied analysis lacks the underlying numeric scores; these findings should therefore remain qualitative.

## Open research directions

1. **Long-duration identity and state stability** — `2607.18217`, `2607.09759`, `2607.17250`: evaluate progressively longer videos or simulations while measuring identity swaps, stale facts, contradictory updates, storage growth, and retrieval latency.

2. **Human validation of model-mediated supervision and judging** — `2607.09759`, `2607.16900`, `2607.17250`, `2607.18110`, `2607.17524`: conduct blinded human audits of memory answers, simulated API transitions, literary trajectories, coached responses, and factual summaries; report agreement and ranking stability across judges.

3. **Matched-seed and matched-tuning RL comparisons** — `2607.16850`, `2607.17247`, `2607.17423`: run multiple seeds with equal hyperparameter-search and compute budgets, reporting mean performance, confidence intervals, reward-group statistics, and training stability.

4. **Teacher- or coach-competence gating** — `2607.17247`, `2607.18110`: compare fixed guidance with verifier-checked, confidence-weighted, or occasional teacher-rollout gating, measuring both target-task gains and capability retention.

5. **Complex stateful tool simulation** — `2607.16900`: execute matched simulated and real write-call sequences, then compare subsequent reads, state invariants, and final task completion; oversample three-app tasks to test whether greater complexity reduces synthesis yield.

6. **VLA generalization beyond seen embodiments and simulators** — `2607.17977`, `2607.09655`, `2607.16074`: hold out a robot embodiment or driving-policy family, train with shared action representations or synthetic data, and evaluate zero-shot and low-shot transfer with repeated physical or closed-loop trials.

7. **End-to-end systems Pareto fronts** — `2607.16074`, `2607.18171`, `2607.18213`: jointly measure latency, throughput, GPU-hours, energy, monetary cost, final task quality, and tail behavior; include deployment-search and pruning overhead rather than reporting tokens or NFE alone.

8. **Causal component isolation in conditioned generation** — `2607.18217`, `2607.09655`, `2607.13365`: use factorial ablations for MLLM guidance/reference embeddings, geometric warping/memory/pose smoothing, and TSDF/extraction/augmentation while holding backbones and data budgets fixed.

9. **Cross-modal testing of inference-time diffusion correction** — `2607.17972`, `2607.18217`: test DiFA on a fixed HOMIE video backbone at equal NFE, measuring identity consistency, temporal artifacts, subject fidelity, latency, and peak memory. This is a proposed experiment; DiFA’s current evidence is image-only.

10. **Human-grounded faithful-generation evaluation** — `2607.17524`, `2607.18110`: supplement model-based factuality and preference scores with blinded human ratings of correctness, coverage, usefulness, and style, controlling for response length and benchmark contamination.
