# Hugging Face Daily Papers — 2026-07-15

## Executive summary

- Repository preparation was a recurring theme: function-aware mid-training improved several Qwen-based SWE-bench agents (2607.12463), while targeted repository QA raised DeepSeek-V3.2 Pass@1 from 66.4% to 70.8% on all 500 SWE-bench Verified issues (2607.11111).
- Two text-to-image studies addressed fidelity through different signals. Selective retrieval reduced damage from unnecessary search (2607.05382), while image-conditioned prompt likelihood served as a zero-shot RL reward across two generator backbones (2607.11886).
- Controlled evaluations exposed weaknesses hidden by aggregate scores: long-document cross-modal reasoning (2607.10400), curated multimodal blind spots (2607.08317), and scientific modeling and mechanistic reasoning (2607.11079).
- Document-native visual pretraining improved controlled OCR and document-understanding transfers, though the resulting parser still trailed specialized systems on OmniDocBench (2607.11562).
- Internal activation probes provided better-calibrated confidence in several forecasting settings and enabled 30–47% token savings through pre-reasoning routing without a detected accuracy loss (2607.08046).
- Domain-focused systems covered autonomous medical-imaging development (2607.10522), multi-instrument transcription (2607.08168), misleading-chart QA (2603.28583), and executable multi-scene Unity generation (2607.11594).
- A frozen image editor handled 25 vision tasks through an RGB interface (2607.12450), but performance ranged from near-specialist depth estimation under alignment-assisted evaluation to weak oracle-assisted detection.
- Deep-RL rankings were shown theoretically capable of reversing across data regimes, with supporting ALE evidence; the result argues for multi-budget evaluation and direct core baselines rather than a universal algorithm ordering (2607.07769).

## Main results worth noting

- **Coding agents:** With otherwise matched R2E-Gym post-training, function-aware FIM raised Qwen2.5-Coder-14B from 26.20% to 29.20% on SWE-bench Verified and from 18.00% to 22.00% on Lite; these Table 1 values were reported as three-seed means (2607.12463). The same intervention did not improve the identified 71 multi-file Verified tasks, which remained near 11.3%.

- **Repository QA:** On all 500 SWE-bench Verified issues with DeepSeek-V3.2, two ACQUIRE QA pairs raised Pass@1 from 66.4% to 70.8%. The intervention produced 44 Fail→Pass and 22 Pass→Fail transitions; only five regressions were judged primarily misleading due to QA (2607.11111).

- **Selective generation search:** On Qwen-Image-2, blind retrieval lowered the 100-prompt NoSearch score from 70.7 to 60.4, whereas ReasonedSearch raised it to 76.5. On the 264-prompt TextualSearch stratum, ReasonedSearch raised the score from 22.9 to 34.1 (2607.05382).

- **Likelihood-based image reward:** At 512 inference on BAGEL, the baseline scored 84.0 GenEval and 75.2/78.6 TIIF-short/long; SpectraReward scored 88.3 and 85.2/84.8, while Self-SpectraReward scored 89.5 and 85.1/84.3. These are benchmark-specific results, not a universal ordering between the reward variants (2607.11886).

- **Long-document diagnosis:** Across 200 synthetic reports and 1,788 questions, Gemini-3.1-Pro achieved 0.725 threshold accuracy and a 7.19 mean judge score. Cross-modal ACC was 0.628 and lower than its chart and complex subsets; results depended materially on page grouping and resolution (2607.10400).

- **Fine-grained blind spots:** GPT-5.5 achieved 84.0% ± 3.2 mean@4 on text-only questions, while Gemini-3.1-Pro achieved 66.9% ± 6.4 on multi-to-text. Fine-grained perception remained weak: the best reported perceptual-counting result was 57.14% over 21 questions (2607.08317).

- **Medical development agents:** Under the authors’ 24-hour, one-RTX-A6000-per-task setup, AMID beat the strongest listed autonomous MLE baseline on 19 of 20 ReX-MLE tasks and tied on TopCoW-CTA-Cls. On focused comparisons, DENTEX improved from the best baseline’s 0.08 AP to AMID’s 0.49 AP, but configurations varied across AMID backends (2607.10522).

- **Music transcription:** On Dagstuhl ChoirSet, the final 1.3B MuScriptor reached 80.7 frame F1 versus 51.0 for YourMT3+. With only 1% of the real-data collection, synthetic pretraining raised offset F1 from 9.9 to 33.4; at full real-data scale the corresponding difference narrowed to 41.0 versus 42.3 (2607.08168).

- **Document encoders:** With the same Qwen3-1.7B decoder, training data, and decoding protocol, structure-aware MonkeyOCRv2-B averaged 57.2 across eight document-understanding benchmarks versus 44.0 for OpenVision-B. On OmniDocBench, however, its parser scored 91.57, below PaddleOCR-VL-1.6’s reported 96.33 under materially different pipelines (2607.11562).

- **Internal calibration:** On 3,020 OpenForesight test rollouts, the EF-8B covariance probe achieved ECE 0.044 versus 0.093 for verbalized confidence, with AUROC 0.756 versus 0.758. On pooled AIME/AMC for a DCPO-recipe model, the probe improved AUROC to 0.929 from 0.865 but had worse ECE, 0.143 versus the best non-probe value of 0.125 (2607.08046).

- **Misleading charts:** On the 305-example Misleading ChartQA test set, full ChartCynics reached 74.43% accuracy, 11.15% wrong-due-to-misleader, and 14.42% other errors, versus Qwen3-VL-8B’s 45.57%, 40.00%, and 14.43% (2603.28583).

- **RGB interface:** On DIODE-indoor, RINO’s Qwen editor reached δ1 0.938 versus a cited specialist’s 0.952 under per-image disparity alignment. In contrast, even with oracle COCO classes and counts, RINO achieved only 16.3% box AP50 versus the cited Mask R-CNN value of 63.5% (2607.12450).

- **RL evaluation:** The theoretical analysis constructs settings where lower-capacity approximations win with scarce data and higher-capacity approximations win asymptotically. ALE comparisons supply empirical evidence of changing rankings, but the missing table cells prevent a complete quantitative audit (2607.07769).

- **Scientific analysis:** On held-out SDA-Synth zero-shot OEQ, GPT-5.4 scored 58.33%, Gemini 3.1 Pro 56.88%, and Claude Sonnet 4.6 55.79%. Across displayed domain cells, descriptive tasks averaged 75.0%, compared with 28.8% predictive and 31.1% mechanistic accuracy (2607.11079).

- **Multi-scene worlds:** MAGIC generated executable projects for all 100 synthetic cases and reported portal identification precision/recall/F1 of 0.99/0.95/0.96. Median generation time was 33.35 minutes per scene, and the evaluation covered only one to five indoor Unity scenes with one run per case (2607.11594).

## Open research directions

1. **Separate persistent repository preparation from issue-time retrieval.** For 2607.12463 and 2607.11111, run a factorial SWE-bench experiment with neither method, FIM only, ACQUIRE only, and both, using identical backbones, post-training, budgets, and repeated trials. Report results by single-function, within-file multi-function, and multi-file patch topology.

2. **Measure fidelity when search and reward are combined.** For 2607.05382 and 2607.11886, compare no search, selective search, likelihood reward, and their combination on the same generator and prompt strata. Track knowledge fidelity, unnecessary-search corruption, aesthetics, safety, reward hacking, and retrieval cost.

3. **Test whether diagnostic failures transfer beyond synthetic construction.** For 2607.10400 and 2607.11079, build matched synthetic and real examples with equivalent evidence chains or scientific relationships. Compare per-factor rankings and error taxonomies rather than only aggregate accuracy.

4. **Establish stable multimodal evaluation.** For 2607.08317, 2607.10400, and 2607.11079, repeat evaluations with multiple graders and blinded human adjudication. For image generation, add repeated samples; for long documents, sweep packaging under a fixed pixel budget.

5. **Quantify visual/OCR error propagation in chart reasoning.** For 2603.28583 and 2607.11562, substitute document-native encoders and inject controlled crop omissions, OCR substitutions, and entity misalignments. Measure chart Acc, WM, WO, latency, and robustness across unseen deception types.

6. **Distinguish calibrated internal signals from causal mechanisms.** For 2607.08046, evaluate probe transfer across models and domains without retraining, then intervene on decoded features. Compare calibration and behavior with simple correlational probes, and test the proposed entropy-gated retrieval branch.

7. **Evaluate domain agents beyond benchmark acceptance.** For 2607.10522, ablate planning, reviewer gates, and two-stage optimization under matched seeds and budgets, then lock generated medical models for external-site and acquisition-shift testing with calibration and leakage audits.

8. **Remove simplifying representational and oracle assumptions.** For 2607.08168, test a tokenizer that represents concurrent same-pitch notes on unfiltered annotations. For 2607.12450, remove oracle classes, counts, boxes, and ground-truth alignment one at a time and report the resulting degradation.

9. **Adopt multi-budget learning-curve evaluation in RL.** For 2607.07769, compare Q-learning, policy-gradient, actor–critic, and model-based methods at logarithmically spaced interaction budgets across multiple domains, with identical seeds and core baselines. Test whether early-curve extrapolation predicts later rankings.

10. **Scale executable world evaluation.** For 2607.11594, repeat trials across seeds, models, engines, and asset libraries at 10, 25, and 50 scenes. Replace or supplement 2D flood fill with collider-aware navigation and report connectivity, traversal success, runtime, and semantic portal fidelity.
