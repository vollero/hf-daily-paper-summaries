# HOMIE: Human-object Centric Video Personalization via Multimodal Intelligent Enchancement

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-21.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.18217) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18217)


## One-sentence takeaway

HOMIE preserves a text encoder while injecting MLLM-derived reference relationships into a video diffusion transformer, improving human–object interaction, logo placement, OCR fidelity, and multi-view personalization across inter- and intra-subject settings.

## Problem and motivation

Human-object-centric video personalization must preserve referenced humans and objects while generating prompt-consistent interactions. The authors identify two gaps: existing inter-subject methods struggle as subject counts grow and cannot reliably infer implicit relationships such as where a logo belongs, while intra-subject methods often fail to recognize that OCR maps or multiple views describe the same object—or require supervised 3D priors (Introduction; Fig. 1).

The authors argue that common MLLM integrations are also limiting: mapping MLLM features into the text-encoder space may constrain their relational information, whereas replacing the text encoder requires costly realignment and makes the MLLM relearn text-to-video control (Introduction; Fig. 2).

## Approach and contributions

- **Unified HOCVP framework:** HOMIE handles distinct inter-subject references and multiple intra-subject references within one DiT-based generation architecture (Sec. 3.1; Fig. 3).

- **Preserved text control with separate MLLM reasoning:** Prompts remain encoded through the original text encoder, while Qwen3-VL-2B-Thinking processes prompts and reference images to extract relational features. Video, reference-image, and aligned MLLM tokens form a unified self-attention stream (Secs. 3 and 3.1.1).

- **Global Multimodal Guidance (GMG):** Pooled MLLM query/key representations generate affine scale-and-shift parameters for video-token queries and keys, injecting global semantic guidance into every DiT block without altering reference-token queries and keys (Sec. 3.1.2; Appendix A.1.1).

- **Modality-Reference Embedding (MRE):** Learnable modality embeddings distinguish video, reference, and MLLM tokens; reference embeddings assign the same identity marker to intra-subject inputs and different markers to inter-subject inputs (Sec. 3.1.3).

- **Curated training and evaluation data:** Training subsets include 300K single-subject samples, 100K multi-subject samples after adding 20K curated clips, and approximately 40K 720p samples. Evaluation uses 200 curated human-object combinations spanning inter-subject, logo, OCR-map, and multi-view cases (Sec. 3.2; Appendix A.2.2).

These results support the authors’ claim that the proposed integration improves several complementary aspects of HOCVP, particularly object similarity, OCR preservation, and multi-view coverage. The component ablations provide evidence that GMG and MRE contribute beyond merely adding MLLM features.

They do not establish universal state of the art: evaluation uses one self-curated 200-sample benchmark, backbone families differ for some comparisons, HOMIE does not win every metric, and no uncertainty estimates or significance tests are reported. Qualitative logo-reasoning claims in Figs. 4, 6, and 10 are illustrative rather than a dedicated quantitative reasoning benchmark.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated limitation:** HOMIE inherits Wan-T2V-14B’s approximately five-second duration limit; long-video applicability remains untested (Appendix A.5.3).

- **Author-stated risk:** Personalized human video generation may be misused for deepfakes. The authors propose compliance requirements for future releases but report no technical mitigation evaluation (Appendix A.5.2).

- **Analyst inference — narrow and self-curated evaluation:** All headline metrics come from 200 curated human-object samples, including 25 logo cases, 60 OCR cases, and 40 multi-view cases. Generalization beyond these compositions is not established (Appendix A.2.2).

- **Analyst inference — comparison confounds:** Although many baselines use Wan-14B, UniVideo and VINO use HunyuanVideo-13B and Kling is closed-source. Backbone, training-data, and inference differences prevent clean architectural attribution (Sec. 4; Appendix Table 7).

- **Analyst inference — metric validity:** Object similarity and OCR extraction depend on GPT-5.2, but no validation against human labels or conventional OCR systems is reported. The overall score averages 16 sampled frames, potentially obscuring temporal failures (Appendix A.2.3).

- **Analyst inference — incomplete statistical reporting:** The text provides no confidence intervals, variance across seeds, significance tests, or numerical results from the user-study figure.

- **Analyst inference — reproducibility burden:** Training requires about 10K A100-hours for HOMIE-Wan2.1, while the main text reports approximately 11K GPU-hours overall. The evaluation set is only promised for later release, and code/model-weight availability is not established (Sec. 4; Appendix Tables 5–6 and A.2.2).

## Open research questions

1. **Can the approach maintain identities and interactions in longer videos?** The current model is limited to roughly five seconds. A next experiment could extend HOMIE with a long-video backbone and measure identity drift, interaction correctness, and temporal continuity over increasing durations.

2. **How robust is implicit logo placement at scale?** Evidence is primarily qualitative, and only 25 evaluation samples cover abstract concepts. A larger benchmark could systematically vary logo type, target ambiguity, occlusion, object count, and prompt explicitness, then score both placement and rendering fidelity.

3. **Does MRE generalize to richer intra-subject evidence?** The paper evaluates OCR maps and multi-view images but not other correlated references. A controlled experiment could add sketches, depth maps, segmentation masks, or close-up details and compare shared versus distinct reference embeddings.

4. **How much performance comes from architecture versus data and backbone choice?** Existing ablations isolate GMG and MRE but do not report matched-data comparisons across backbones or MLLMs. Training identical configurations with different MLLMs, frozen versus tuned feature extractors, and fixed data budgets would clarify causal contributions.
