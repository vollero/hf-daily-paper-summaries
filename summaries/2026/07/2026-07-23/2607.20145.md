# SLAI T-Rex: Full-Parameter Post-training of the DeepSeek-V4 Family on Ascend SuperPOD

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-23.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.20145) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20145)


## One-sentence takeaway

The authors combine Ascend-native distributed and kernel optimizations with solver-grounded continued pre-training and supervised fine-tuning, reporting 34.22% MFU for full-parameter DeepSeek-V4-Pro training and stronger zero-shot operations-research modeling with DeepSeek-V4-Flash.

## Problem and motivation

Full-parameter post-training of trillion-parameter MoE models creates severe memory, communication, pipeline-bubble, and irregular-kernel bottlenecks, while existing large-scale systems are primarily designed for CUDA or TPU hardware rather than SIMD-oriented Ascend NPUs (Sections 1 and 2.1).

The authors also identify a domain gap: LLM-based operations-research work emphasizes agent pipelines more than parameter-level specialization, despite persistent failures in executable solver code, discrete-variable semantics, nonlinear reformulation, and canonical constraint–variable structure (Sections 1 and 3.1).

## Approach and contributions

- **Ascend-native training stack:** Hierarchical optimization of parallelism, computation–communication overlap, memory movement, optimizer-state swapping, and kernel execution for full-parameter DeepSeek-V4-Pro training (Sections 2.1–2.3; Figure 3).
- **AuraKernel:** An AscendC optimization agent combining solver-guided tiling, correctness-gated hardware profiling, branchable K-Search, rollback, and hardware-verified skill distillation (Section 2.3.2; Figure 7).
- **Hardware-aware fusion and rewriting:** Eager operator chains and generic Triton kernels are consolidated into Unified-Buffer-resident AscendC kernels, including mHC, limited-SwiGLU backward, and rotary embedding paths (Section 2.3.3; Figures 8–11).
- **OR-oriented CPT–SFT pipeline:** Solver-verified bidirectional CPT synthesis, self-distilled SFT data, leakage controls, contract-aware validation, and matched SFT-only versus CPT-plus-SFT evaluation (Sections 3.2–3.3; Figures 13–14).
- **Provenance and monitoring:** Stage manifests connect CPT, SFT, weight conversion, deployment, and evaluation; a dashboard tracks training health and capability retention (Appendix D–E).

These results support the authors’ claims that coordinated system optimization improves Ascend training efficiency and that CPT contributes additional OR transfer beyond matched SFT alone. They do not establish portability to other models or accelerators, nor do the visible excerpts provide the full statistical analysis, repeated-run variance, complete baseline configurations, or all Section 4 ablations.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Source truncation:** Approximately 92,523 characters from the middle—including much of Sections 3.3–5 and the central experimental tables—were omitted. Coverage is therefore incomplete.
- **Author-stated modeling limitation:** Matching a solver objective on one instance is not proof of symbolic equivalence; structurally different formulations may share an optimum (Appendix C.2, Equation 18 discussion).
- **Author-stated performance-model limitation:** AuraKernel’s L2 cache-hit modeling is coarse and causes residual prediction error for some shapes (Section 2.3.2).
- **Author-stated scheduling scope:** VPP was selected over DualPipeV for the profiled workload, but the authors leave optimal pipeline scheduling to future study (Section 2.2).
- **Analyst inference — hardware generality:** Results are tied to Ascend 910C, AscendC, and SuperPOD topology; no evidence in the supplied text shows transfer to GPUs, TPUs, or other NPU generations.
- **Analyst inference — evaluation breadth:** Domain results use four OR benchmarks and Gurobi-facing contracts. This does not establish performance across other solvers or broader industrial deployments.
- **Analyst inference — reproducibility:** Recipes are partially reported in Tables 16–17, but environment-specific launch details, dataset allocation, checkpoint paths, and some configurations are explicitly omitted.
- **Analyst inference — comparison uncertainty:** The full baseline settings, activated-parameter accounting, uncertainty estimates, and detailed GPT-5.4-Mini evaluation protocol fall inside the missing material or are not visible.

## Open research questions

1. **How much does each systems layer contribute independently?**  
   The visible text gives an aggregate MFU gain but not the full component ablation. A next experiment could add parallelism, overlap, swapping, AuraKernel, and fusion one at a time under an identical workload.

2. **Do AuraKernel’s learned skills transfer across operators and shapes?**  
   The paper describes reusable skill distillation, but cross-operator and cross-shape transfer evidence is unavailable here. A held-out evaluation could compare search cost and final latency with versus without retrieved skills.

3. **Does CPT improve OR structure beyond the four reported benchmarks?**  
   Matched CPT→SFT gains are shown, especially for B4O-ORGEval, but broader structural generalization is unclear. A next experiment could evaluate unseen OR families, shifted schemas, and alternative solvers.

4. **How robust is solver verification to equivalent formulations?**  
   Objective agreement can admit structurally different models, while strict graph matching can reject valid alternatives. Future evaluation could combine multi-instance testing, symbolic equivalence checks, and semantic constraint audits.

## Confidence and missing information

**Confidence: medium.** The supplied pages support the central method, diagnostic profile, recipes, and headline numbers, but the source is substantially truncated. Missing material includes most detailed Section 4 results and ablations, the complete SFT construction and cleaning analysis, full baseline configurations, variance or significance estimates, general-capability retention results, complete dataset composition and release terms, and the authors’ full limitations and conclusion.
