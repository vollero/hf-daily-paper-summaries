# SeededGrasp: Language-Guided Grasping in Complex Scenes with Multiple Embodiments

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-23.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.20207) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20207)


## One-sentence takeaway

SeededGrasp decouples language-based target-part selection from geometric grasp generation via a VLM-predicted 3D seed point, achieving roughly 72% simulated and 78% real-world grasp success while supporting three training embodiments.

## Problem and motivation

The paper addresses task-aware robotic grasping in cluttered scenes across different end-effectors. This requires jointly interpreting language, reasoning about occluded 3D geometry, avoiding surrounding objects, and producing embodiment-specific poses.

The authors identify a gap between approaches that ask VLMs to predict grasps with limited spatial precision and end-to-end vision-language-grasp systems requiring large annotated datasets and substantial compute. They also argue that existing datasets do not combine cluttered multi-object scenes with multi-embodiment grasp poses (Sections 1–2).

## Approach and contributions

1. **Seed-point interface.** An off-the-shelf VLM zero-shot selects a task-relevant point in a bird’s-eye-view image; that point is projected into the scene point cloud and conditions grasp generation (Sections 4 and 4.3).

2. **Multi-embodiment grasp generator.** A conditional flow-matching DiT generates translation, rotation, and joint configuration from the seed, scene point cloud, gripper point cloud, and a learned gripper query (Section 4.1). Language grounding and geometric execution are therefore trained separately.

3. **Synthetic dataset.** The authors release 2.56M filtered grasps across 610 cluttered scenes, 334 objects, and three grippers: Franka Panda, Robotiq 3-Finger, and Allegro (Sections 1 and 3).

4. **Evaluation across several axes.** Experiments cover seed selection, conditional generation, multi-embodiment performance, language-conditioned grasping, dataset and representation ablations, and sim-to-real transfer (Section 5).

The results establish strong performance within the authors’ simulation protocol and a small physical test, and they support the usefulness of seed conditioning. They do not establish a single uniformly controlled comparison across all capabilities: the baselines support different embodiments and inputs, and Geomatch was tested with masks in single-object scenes. Missing numerical contents for Tables 3–7 and several ablations prevent independent assessment of some reported advantages.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated:** Generated poses ignore robot-arm kinematics and obstacles, so they may be unreachable even when the hand pose itself is valid (Section 6).
- **Author-stated:** A single BEV image limits robustness when useful grasp regions are occluded; multi-view VLM conditioning remains unexplored (Section 6).
- **Author-stated:** Learned embodiment-specific queries limit transfer to unseen hardware (Section 6).
- **Author-stated:** MGG over-represents power grasps, reducing effectiveness on flat objects resting flush on the table (Section 6).
- **Author-stated:** Real-world failures often arose from excessive hand-object distance, sometimes amplified by angled grasps; the untrained Delto hand also differs geometrically from the Robotiq hand used to generate predictions (Sections 5.3 and A.7).
- **Analyst inference:** Dataset grasps are accepted through angle, clearance, and interpenetration heuristics rather than dynamic in-scene lift tests, so label validity may differ from execution success (Section 3).
- **Analyst inference:** Baseline comparability is limited: DGN2.0 is single-embodiment; Geomatch received masks and single-object scenes; ShapeGrasp and GraspMAS use different pipelines and, for GraspMAS, a different VLM (Appendix A.2).
- **Analyst inference:** Language adherence was manually scored, but the supplied text does not describe annotator count, agreement, or blinding (Section 5.1.3).
- **Analyst inference:** The physical evaluation covers only 15 objects and one mapped three-finger gripper, without a reported real-world baseline or uncertainty estimate (Section 5.3).

## Open research questions

1. **Can seed-conditioned grasps be made arm-reachable and collision-free?** Arm kinematics are currently omitted. A next experiment could jointly condition pose generation on a specific arm model and compare executed success against post-hoc motion-planning rejection.

2. **Does multi-view grounding improve performance under occlusion?** The current VLM uses one BEV image. A controlled study could vary object occlusion while comparing single-view, multi-view, and directly 3D-grounded seed prediction.

3. **Can the model generalize natively to unseen end-effectors?** The Delto experiment requires fixed manual offsets from Robotiq predictions. A next experiment could replace learned gripper IDs with a morphology or joint-space representation and test held-out grippers without manual mapping.

4. **How reliable are heuristically filtered grasp labels?** Millions of grasps are filtered without dynamic scene lift tests. A stratified validation could execute sampled accepted and rejected grasps in simulation, measuring false-positive and false-negative rates by gripper and scene density.
