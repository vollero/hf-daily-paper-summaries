# Audio-Visual Flamingo: Open Audio-Visual Intelligence for Long and Complex Videos

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-20.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.16107) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16107)


## One-sentence takeaway

AV-Flamingo combines large-scale cross-modal training data, a short-to-long-context curriculum, and timestamp-grounded reasoning to improve open audio-visual understanding of videos up to 15 minutes, though the retrieved results omit many full comparison tables and ablations.

## Problem and motivation

Most video-language models either omit audio or concentrate on short clips, despite real-world videos combining speech, sound, music, and visuals over extended timelines. The authors identify three related gaps: scarce large-scale paired audio-visual reasoning data, weak explicit temporal and cross-modal training, and limited openness around the strongest systems’ data and methods (Section 1).

## Approach and contributions

- **AV-Flamingo model:** A 7B-parameter Qwen2.5-based model with separate SigLip vision and AF-Whisper audio encoders. Audio and visual tokens are synchronized, temporally interleaved, augmented with Constrained Rotary Time Embeddings, and passed to the language model (Section 2.1; Figure 2).
- **AV-Skills dataset:** Approximately 7M caption and QA instances spanning short videos up to 60 seconds and long videos from 60 seconds to 15 minutes. It targets temporal, compositional, counting, referring, alignment, and holistic cross-modal reasoning (Section 2.2).
- **Three-stage curriculum:** Short-context pre-training, long-context mid-training, and post-training with supervised fine-tuning followed by GRPO-based reinforcement learning. Context grows from 16K to 32K tokens, producing AVF-Instruct and AVF-Think (Section 2.3; Figure 2).
- **TAVIT and AV-Think:** Temporal Audio-Visual Interleaved Chain-of-Thought grounds intermediate audio-visual reasoning to timestamps. Its training set contains approximately 24K examples with chains averaging 635.7 words (Section 2.2).
- **Open release:** The authors state that the model, training and inference code, dataset, and associated techniques are released (Abstract; Section 1).

These results support broad transfer across audio, speech, and video tasks and provide several direct baseline comparisons. They do not isolate the effects of AV-Skills, the curriculum, TAVIT, SFT, or GRPO because the referenced ablation tables are absent from the retrieved text. Exact WorldSense, DailyOmni, MMOU, LongVideoBench, and most baseline scores are also unavailable, so broader “state-of-the-art” claims cannot be independently checked from this text alone.

## Limitations and validity risks

The authors acknowledge that AV-Skills may contain source bias and overlap with prior training data because it draws from public datasets and open-internet videos. They also report continuing difficulty with very long, dense videos—especially when evidence is sparse or widely dispersed—and note that current benchmarks inadequately represent open-ended deployment (Section 5).

**Analyst inferences:**

- The maximum 15-minute training duration limits evidence for videos longer than that, despite broader references to long-form content (Sections 2.2–2.3).
- Synthetic annotation of internet videos and LLM-generated AV-Think reasoning may introduce annotation or rationale errors; the supplied text does not report human validation rates (Section 2.2).
- The missing appendix tables prevent assessment of data mixtures, hyperparameters, curriculum ablations, and component-level causal attribution.
- Training used 512 H100 GPUs, creating a substantial replication barrier even though code and models are reportedly open (Section 3).
- Timestamped chain-of-thought may improve interpretability, but the supplied results do not quantify timestamp accuracy, rationale faithfulness, or TAVIT’s incremental benefit.

## Open research questions

1. **How much does each training stage contribute?** The retrieved text references but does not provide the curriculum ablation. A next experiment could compare initialization-only, short-context, long-context, TAVIT-SFT, and GRPO checkpoints under identical benchmarks.

2. **Are timestamped rationales faithful to model decisions?** No rationale-faithfulness or temporal-grounding metric is reported. A next experiment could perturb or mask the cited intervals and measure whether answers change consistently with the stated evidence.

3. **Does performance extend beyond 15-minute videos?** Training is capped at 15 minutes and 32K tokens, while very long dense videos remain difficult. A length-controlled evaluation could test matched tasks across progressively longer videos and varying evidence sparsity.

4. **How robust is the model to modality conflict or absence?** The architecture accepts either or both modalities, but controlled conflict results are not supplied. An experiment could compare audio-only, visual-only, aligned audio-visual, misaligned, and deliberately contradictory inputs.
