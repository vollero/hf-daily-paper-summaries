# Qwen-Music Technical Report

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-20.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.11699) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11699)


## One-sentence takeaway

Qwen-Music separates long-range semantic composition from 48 kHz stereo rendering and, on the authors’ bilingual evaluations, combines strong text-to-song quality with reference-melody cover generation, though several training-component gains and proprietary-system comparisons remain incompletely isolated.

## Problem and motivation

Full-song generation must jointly sustain lyrics, melody, rhythm, vocals, instrumentation, and section structure over minutes while producing detailed, natural stereo audio. The authors identify a mismatch between these levels: compact discrete tokens make long-range autoregressive composition tractable but discard acoustic detail, whereas waveform-level rendering must recover timbre, phase, spatial placement, and high frequencies. Cover generation adds the competing requirements of preserving a reference melody while changing style, arrangement, and voice (Section 1).

## Approach and contributions

- **Semantic–acoustic decomposition:** A three-component pipeline uses Qwen-Music-Tokenizer for compact music representation, Qwen-Music-LLM for autoregressive composition, and Qwen-Music-Render for waveform synthesis (Sections 2.1–2.4; Figure 4).
- **Music Semantic Tokens:** A four-stage, 0.6B-parameter Conformer tokenizer produces one token every 40 ms from a single 32,768-entry codebook, corresponding to 25 Hz and 375 bit/s (Section 2.2; Table 1).
- **Melody-CoT:** Relative-pitch melody tokens explicitly plan vocal contours for original songs or condition cover generation. Section-level and unique-section-level variants trade direct melody preservation against tag adherence (Sections 2.3.1–2.3.2).
- **Generative stereo renderer:** A 1.3B-parameter DiT predicts continuous acoustic latents; Spec-VAE reconstructs complex spectrograms; Spec-SnakeBeta and a frequency-specific Band-Mode Refiner target magnitude, phase, and stereo errors before 48 kHz synthesis (Section 2.4; Figures 6–7).
- **Large-scale training:** The LLM uses more than five million hours of multilingual music, a genre-normalized quality curriculum, and progressive SFT, offline DPO, and online GSPO alignment (Section 3).
- **Unified task support:** The same framework handles prompt-and-lyrics-conditioned song creation and reference-audio-conditioned cover generation (Sections 1 and 2.3).

The evidence supports strong performance under the authors’ bilingual prompt sets, professional preference protocol, two cover-reference sets, and reconstruction benchmark. It does not establish universal superiority: Qwen-Music does not lead every metric, the Suno V5.5 preference difference is only 0.6 percentage points, no significance analysis is supplied for A/B win rates, and real-song cover comparison excludes Suno because its service did not accept those inputs.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated future gaps:** The conclusion calls for more flexible long-context structure modeling, finer expressive control over singing and performance, and more efficient rendering architectures (Section 5).
- **Analyst inference — limited causal attribution:** No reported ablation isolates Melody-CoT against no melody planning, the quality-graded curriculum, SFT versus DPO versus GSPO, or the tokenizer’s four training stages. Their individual contribution to final quality is therefore not established.
- **Analyst inference — evaluator dependence:** Many “objective” song-quality results depend on learned evaluators, including SongBench, SongEval, AudioBox-Aesthetic, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Qwen3-ASR (Section 4.1). Agreement with human perception or robustness to evaluator bias is not analyzed in the supplied text.
- **Analyst inference — comparison asymmetry:** Proprietary baselines have undisclosed internals and potentially different native operating conditions. Suno cannot be evaluated on the real-song reference set, narrowing the strongest cover comparison (Section 4.1).
- **Analyst inference — linguistic and genre scope:** Training spans hundreds of languages, but reported generation tests cover only Chinese and English; genre-stratified human results cover eight categories (Tables 4–8).
- **Analyst inference — reproducibility:** The text does not provide model checkpoints, code, data provenance, licensing, compute cost, full hyperparameters, prompt set release details, or training-set contamination analysis.

## Open research questions

1. **How much does explicit Melody-CoT improve original-song structure?** The paper compares its two conditioning granularities for covers but not Melody-CoT against direct text-to-music generation. A controlled ablation could hold model, renderer, prompts, and sampling fixed while measuring structure, memorability, and expert preference.

2. **Which training stage drives the reported gains?** Curriculum pre-training, SFT, iterative DPO, and GSPO are presented as one pipeline without stage-by-stage results. Evaluating successive checkpoints on the same bilingual and human-rated suite would quantify marginal gains and regressions.

3. **Can melody fidelity and style control be improved simultaneously?** Section-level conditioning lowers Melody MAE, whereas unique-section conditioning generally improves tags and intelligibility (Tables 6–7). A next experiment could vary melody-token rate, section sampling, or conditioning strength and estimate the Pareto frontier.

4. **How well does the system generalize beyond Chinese and English and beyond six-minute training tracks?** The corpus is broadly multilingual, but evaluation is bilingual and DiT training caps tracks at six minutes. Matched multilingual and long-form test sets could measure lyric accuracy, structure, and acoustic stability by language and duration.

## Confidence and missing information

**Confidence: high** for the reported architecture, evaluation settings, and tabulated results because the supplied text is detailed and complete.

Material missing information includes public-release status; code and checkpoint availability; training-data sources, licenses, and deduplication; total compute and inference speed; full optimization and sampling settings; parameter count for the complete system; confidence intervals or significance tests for human preferences; evaluator validation; safety and copyright analysis; contamination checks; and controlled ablations for Melody-CoT, tokenizer stages, curriculum learning, DPO, and GSPO.
