# On-Policy Delta Distillation

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-20.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.15161) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15161)


## One-sentence takeaway

The authors propose OPD 2, which distills the probability change between a reasoning-tuned teacher and its base checkpoint, and report more reliable reasoning gains than conventional OPD across Qwen3 and Gemma4 evaluations, at modest additional training cost.

## Problem and motivation

On-policy distillation (OPD) trains an LLM on its own sampled tokens using dense teacher-provided rewards, avoiding domain-specific reward-model design. Conventional OPD rewards agreement with the teacher’s full output distribution, which may transfer pre-existing stylistic or token preferences alongside reasoning ability.

The authors argue that OPD’s reward design is underexplored and that the change induced by reasoning post-training—the difference between a tuned teacher and its base checkpoint—could provide a more targeted reasoning signal (Introduction; Fig. 1). This matters particularly for already-capable reasoning models, where standard OPD sometimes degrades performance (Tables 4–7).

## Approach and contributions

1. **Delta signal:** For each student-sampled token, OPD 2 uses the log-probability difference between a reasoning-tuned teacher and its pre-post-training base model, rather than the conventional teacher–student difference (Eqs. 4–5).

2. **Centered, conditionally applied reward:** The method centers both conventional and delta rewards under the student distribution, approximating the expectation over the student’s top 1,024 tokens (Eqs. 6–7). It applies the delta advantage only when its sign agrees with the conventional OPD advantage, preventing updates that move the student away from the teacher and providing a teacher-matching stopping point (Eqs. 8–9).

3. **Signal analysis:** Word-cloud, curated token-level, and corpus-level analyses suggest that delta rewards differ systematically from conventional OPD rewards, including stronger signals on some logical connective tokens and stronger suppression of some uncertain or generic narration tokens (Figs. 2–3; Table 1). These interpretations are primarily author claims rather than direct demonstrations of improved reasoning correctness.

4. **Multi-domain evaluation:** OPD 2 is compared with untrained checkpoints, standard OPD, and ExOPD on 14 math, code, and science benchmarks, covering Qwen3 students from 1.7B to 8B in thinking and non-thinking modes and Gemma4-E4B-it in thinking mode (Section 3; Tables 2–7).

The results establish that, under the authors’ 100-step training recipe and benchmark suite, OPD 2 generally produces higher final-step averages than OPD and ExOPD. The ablation supports the delta signal as the principal empirical contributor.

They do not establish statistical significance, robustness across random training seeds, superiority outside the two tested model families, or universal capability preservation. In particular, Gemma4 code performance remained below the original model despite OPD 2 being the least damaging distillation variant (Table 7).

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated convergence concern:** A pure delta reward ignores the student distribution in its reward calculation and tends theoretically toward a one-hot maximum-reward token; OPD 2 introduces centering and sign gating to mitigate this (Section 2.3).

- **Author-stated compute cost:** A teacher-base forward pass adds 8–28% wall-clock time over OPD in the reported configurations, and the implementation is described as unoptimized (Section 3.5; Table 9).

- **Author-observed late-training degradation:** OPD variants can peak early and then plateau or deteriorate. Results use the final step, but this makes training duration and checkpoint selection consequential (Section 3.3; Fig. 4).

- **Analyst inference — limited model coverage:** Evidence covers only Qwen3 and Gemma4, with Gemma4 tested only in thinking mode. Cross-family generality remains only partially supported (Sections 3.1–3.2).

- **Analyst inference — uncertainty is underreported:** Pass@1 is averaged over repeated stochastic generations, but the supplied text reports no confidence intervals, dispersion, significance tests, or independent training-seed replication (Section 3.1; Table 12).

- **Analyst inference — component effects are not uniformly positive:** Removing centering or gating sometimes matches or exceeds full OPD 2—for example, non-thinking math is 55.8 without gating versus 54.6 for OPD 2—so their general benefit is not conclusively established by Table 8.

- **Analyst inference — mechanistic evidence is suggestive:** The word-level analyses associate selected token shifts with reasoning quality, but they do not directly show that those shifts cause benchmark improvements. Figure 3 uses three externally generated, manually curated examples rather than student rollouts.

- **Analyst inference — reproducibility is incomplete:** Hyperparameters and hardware are detailed, but code is only promised for future availability; the supplied text does not establish released checkpoints, exact sampled training subsets, or training seeds.

## Open research questions

1. **Does the delta signal remain effective when the tuned and base teacher checkpoints are less closely matched?** The experiments use corresponding checkpoints from the same model lineage. A next experiment could vary base checkpoints, post-training recipes, or model families while holding the student and training questions fixed.

2. **Are centering and sign gating reliably beneficial across seeds and training lengths?** Table 8 shows small and sometimes reversed differences. A factorial ablation across multiple training seeds, learning rates, and checkpoints could measure their effects with confidence intervals.

3. **Can OPD 2 preserve capabilities outside the training mixture?** Gemma4 coding declines despite balanced math/science/code training, and no unrelated capability or safety evaluations are reported. A next experiment could evaluate broad language, instruction-following, and safety suites before and after distillation.

4. **Do token-level delta patterns causally explain the gains?** Current signal analyses are correlational or illustrative. A plausible experiment would selectively mask or rescale token categories identified by the analysis and test whether reasoning accuracy changes predictably.
