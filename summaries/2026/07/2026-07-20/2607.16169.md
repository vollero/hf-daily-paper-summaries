# When Does Muon Help Agentic Reinforcement Learning?

> [← Daily summary](../2026-07-20.md) · [Relationship graph](relationships.md) · [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/papers/2607.16169) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16169)


## One-sentence takeaway

In single-seed ALFWorld experiments, applying vanilla Muon to an LLM agent’s hidden matrices improved success and learning efficiency over AdamW, but the magnitude depended strongly on the advantage estimator and learning rate.

## Problem and motivation

Muon is competitive with AdamW in pre-training, but its usefulness for RL post-training is unresolved, with prior evidence ranging from minor gains to instability. The authors identify a gap in long-horizon, sparse-reward agentic RL: negative evidence largely concerns single-turn RL with episode-level credit, leaving unclear whether finer-grained advantage estimation changes Muon’s behavior (Introduction).

This matters because agentic tasks require credit assignment across many actions, and Muon’s spectral normalization may amplify either useful weak gradient directions or noise.

## Approach and contributions

- **Controlled optimizer comparison:** The authors train Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct on ALFWorld for 200 updates, replacing AdamW only for attention and MLP weight matrices with vanilla Muon; embeddings, norms, and other parameters remain on AdamW (Method; Experimental Setup).
- **Estimator comparison:** Matched Muon–AdamW runs cover episode-level GRPO, anchor-state step credit with GiGPO, and graph-based transition credit with GraphGPO (Introduction; Compared Configurations).
- **Step-credit factorial:** A matched GiGPO \(2\times2\) ablation varies optimizer and whether step-level credit is enabled, separating Muon’s effect from GiGPO’s additional credit signal (Table 3).
- **Learning-rate and dynamics analysis:** The study tests two Muon rates for GiGPO and GraphGPO and high-rate AdamW controls for GiGPO (Table 2; Figure 3).
- **Mechanistic conjecture:** A simplified gradient-SNR analysis proposes that finer credit may improve the reliability of weak gradient directions amplified by Muon; the paper explicitly does not measure this mechanism (Method; Appendix D).

The evidence establishes that these particular Muon configurations outperform their matched AdamW runs along individual training trajectories. It also shows that the observed benefit varies by estimator, rate, and whether late success or learning speed is measured.

It does **not** establish population-level reliability, statistical significance, cross-task generality, or the proposed gradient-SNR mechanism. The high-rate AdamW result only rules out those tested nominal rates under GiGPO; Muon and AdamW learning rates are not directly equivalent.

## Limitations and validity risks

- **Author-stated:** Every experiment is single-seed, so the results lack uncertainty estimates and may reflect trajectory variability.
- **Author-stated:** Evaluation covers one 0.5B model and one environment.
- **Author-stated:** Learning-rate coverage is narrow: two Muon rates for GiGPO and GraphGPO, one for GRPO, and high-rate AdamW controls only for GiGPO.
- **Author-stated:** The optimizer-by-step-credit ablation is also single-seed.
- **Author-stated:** Gradient SNR and update spectra are not measured; the proposed mechanism is correlational and relies on simplified assumptions that omit clipping, KL regularization, momentum history, and singular-vector rotation.
- **Author-stated:** Full-matrix `NO_SHARD` increases per-device memory and limits scaling without a distributed Muon implementation.
- **Analyst inference:** Because estimator implementations are compared within one environment and some rate coverage differs by estimator, the experiments cannot cleanly separate estimator structure from estimator-specific tuning.
- **Analyst inference:** Repeated sampled validation checkpoints support trajectory comparisons but do not replace independent training seeds.

## Open research questions

1. **Do the gains persist across random seeds, model scales, and environments?**  
   The paper evaluates one seed, model, and task suite. A next experiment should run matched multi-seed sweeps across several model sizes and agentic benchmarks, reporting confidence intervals.

2. **Does finer-grained credit actually improve Muon-relevant gradient directions?**  
   The SNR explanation is conjectural. A next experiment could log per-layer spectra, effective rank, tail-direction sign agreement, and cross-minibatch SNR for GRPO, GiGPO, and GraphGPO.

3. **What are the optimizer-specific learning-rate response surfaces?**  
   The tested rates are sparse and not directly comparable across optimizers. A factorial sweep over optimizer, estimator, and rate—using identical seeds and budgets—could distinguish robust regions from isolated favorable settings.

4. **Can Muon scale without full-matrix replication?**  
   The reference implementation uses `NO_SHARD`. A next experiment should compare distributed or sharded Muon implementations against the full-matrix update for numerical fidelity, memory, throughput, and RL performance.
